5.3. Draft general budget of the European Union for the financial year 2010 as modified by the Council (vote)

Draft amendments to the draft general budget of the European Union for the financial year 2010 as modified by the Council (all sections)

(Qualified majority)

(Voting record: 'Results of votes', Item 3)

The amendments adopted appear in an annex to the Texts Adopted.

Before the vote, László Surján (rapporteur) made a brief statement and Vladimír Maňka spoke on the voting procedure.

Hans Lindblad (President-in-Office of the Council) made the following statement:

'The Council notes with satisfaction that Parliament has confirmed the agreement we reached at our conciliation meeting of 18 November 2009. We consider that Parliament's second reading, together with the outcome of the Council's second reading, can be considered as forming a joint text by the two branches of the budgetary authority, in accordance with the new Article 314 of the Treaty of Lisbon.

The Coucil accepts this joint text. It therefore falls to the President of the European Parliament to declare that the budget has been definitively adopted. The Council considers, however, that, once the President has declared the budget definitively adopted, it should be signed by both parties, in accordance with the new Treaty.

The Council regrets that this position has not been taken into consideration. That being said, we do not in any way question the outcome of our agreement. We hope, nevetherless, that the matter can be examined before the next budgetary procedure.

Finally, I should like to thank the Chair of the Committee on Budgets Alain Lamassoure and the rapporteurs László Surján, Vladimír Maňka and Jutta Haug for their constructive attitude throughout this procedure, which highlights the very good cooperation we have achieved.'

The President took note and made the following statement:

'The budgetary procedure for 2010 has been exceptional. It started under Article 272 of the EC Treaty, but its last stage has been conducted when the Treaty of Lisbon entered into force.

On 18 November 2009, Parliament, Council and Commission therefore agreed to continue the budgetary procedure under the EC Treaty, but also reached an agreement covering the outcome of the budgetary procedure, including Parliament's vote, which took place in full respect of the Multiannual Financial Framework.

The President of the Council just confirmed the agreement of the Council on the budget we just voted. This means that Parliament and Council now reached an agreement on a joint text, respecting the aim and purpose of Article 314 TFEU.

I thus note that the budgetary procedure, that has been initiated under Article 272 of the Treaty establishing the European Community and continued under Article 314 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, has been completed in accordance with the Article 314 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and the Multiannual Financial Framework.

The budgetary procedure can thus be deemed to be completed and the budget is declared definitively adopted'.

The President invited Hans Lindblad, Algirdas Šemeta (Member of the Commission), Alain Lamassoure (Chair of the BUDG Committee), and the rapporteurs László Surján and Vladimír Maňka to join him.

The President signed the budget.

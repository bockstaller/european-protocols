IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Friday 21 May 2021 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.10.


3. Statement by the President

The President made a statement to mark the return of Parliament’s plenary sitting to its seat in Strasbourg after more than 15 months away owing to the COVID-19 pandemic. He stated that he saw the return as a sign of confidence and hope following the extremely testing times that our societies had endured. He pointed out that, in taking extraordinary measures and adopting new working methods, Parliament had been able to carry out its duties and exercise its prerogatives. He thanked the French authorities and the people of Strasbourg for their support and hospitality, and welcomed the Mayor of Strasbourg, who was in attendance in the Chamber.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

14. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the June I 2021 sittings had been distributed (PE 693.498/PDOJ).

The President informed the House that he had received a request for urgent procedure (Rule 163) in respect of the following legislative file:

- Transitional provisions of certain machinery fitted with engines in the power range between 56kW and 130kW, and above 300kW in order to address the impact of the COVID-19 crisis (item 159 on the final draft agenda).

The request would be put to the vote during that day’s voting session.

After consulting the political groups, the President proposed the following changes to the final draft agenda:

Monday

No changes.

Tuesday

The Council and Commission statements on the European Parliament’s scrutiny on the ongoing assessment by the Commission and the Council of the national recovery and resilience plans (item 96 on the final draft agenda) would be moved to become the third item on the agenda, after the Commission’s statement on the state of play of the implementation of the own resources roadmap (item 97 on the final draft agenda).

The debate on the report by María Soraya Rodríguez Ramos on the 75th and 76th sessions of the United Nations General Assembly (item 31 on the final draft agenda) would be moved to become the fourth item on the agenda in the afternoon, after the statement by the Vice-President of the Commission / High-Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the human rights and political situation in Cuba (item 101 on the final draft agenda).

Wednesday

No changes.

Thursday

No changes.

The votes would be spread over the various voting sessions depending on the number of amendments and requests for split and separate votes. Information on the manner in which the votes would be spread over the various voting sessions would be available on Parliament’s website under ‘Priority information and documents’.

The House agreed to the changes.

The order of business was thus established.

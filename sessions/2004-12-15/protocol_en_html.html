4.1.  Turkey's progress towards accession (vote)

Report on the 2004 report and the recommendation of the European Commission on Turkey’s progress towards accession [COM(2004)0656 - C6-0148/2004 - 2004/2182(INI) ] - Committee on Foreign Affairs. Rapporteur: Camiel Eurlings (A6-0063/2004 ) .

(Simple majority)

(Voting record: Annex 1, Item 1)

MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION

Adopted ( P6_TA(2004)0096 )

(The list of Members taking part in the secret ballot appears in Annex 2, 'Result of roll-call votes'. Signatories to the requests for secret ballots are listed in Annex 3.)

The following spoke on the vote:

Before the vote, Graham Watson , on behalf of the ALDE Group , rose to object to the fact that requests for secret ballots had been made under Rule 162(2); he asked that the President rule the requests inadmissible and also asked for the Rule in question to be clarified.

The following spoke on the request: Daniel Marc Cohn-Bendit , on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group , and Martin Schulz , on behalf of the PSE Group , who both supported Graham Watson 's request; Richard Corbett , who pointed out that a secret ballot was possible, but not mandatory, under Rule 162(2) ('Voting may also be by secret ballot'); Francis Wurtz , on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group , who asked the President to let the House vote on whether a secret ballot should be held (the President did not do so); Vytautas Landsbergis , who asked members of his Group who had signed requests for a secret ballot to withdraw their signatures; Francesco Enrico Speroni , Jacques Toubon and Bernd Posselt , all on the application of Rule 162.

The President then pointed out that three requests for secret ballots had been received, and that these had been made in accordance with the Rules. He also pointed out that he had sought the advice of Parliament's services and had considered relevant precedents, including a decision taken by President Pat Cox on 12 June 2002 concerning the calendar of part-sessions for 2003 (see Annex 1, Minutes of 12.06.2002, Item 7 ) , as well as a judgment of October 2001 by the Court of First Instance in a similar case which found that the word 'may' was, in the context of Article 29(1) of the Rules of Procedure, to be regarded as a clause with mandatory effect.

He thus decided, in the light of these precedents, to hold a secret ballot using the electronic voting system without prejudice to any subsequent decisions reached by the committee responsible.

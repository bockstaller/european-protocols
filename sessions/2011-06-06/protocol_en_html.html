IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

Christine De Veyrac had been present but her name was not on the attendance register.

Charles Tannock informed the Presidency that he wished to withdraw his signature from motion for a resolution B7-0325/2011 tabled by the ECR group.


3. Welcome

On behalf of Parliament, the President welcomed Emomali Rahmon, the President of Tajikistan, who had taken a seat in the distinguished visitors' gallery.


4. Announcements by the President

The President recalled the 22nd anniversary of the student demonstrations in Tiananmen Square, Beijing, by the authorities of the People's Republic of China.

He also recalled the 22nd anniversary of the first free parliamentary elections in Poland, which were won by the democratic opposition.

°
° ° °

The President announced that an exceptional evacuation exercise would take place during the current part-session in Strasbourg, with another scheduled for the June mini-session in Brussels.

°
° ° °

The President announced that Silvana Koch-Mehrin had submitted her resignation as Vice-President of the European Parliament with effect from 11 May 2011. Pursuant to Rule 18 of the Rules of Procedure, Parliament established that the post would be vacant.


5. Verification of credentials

In accordance with Rule 3(3) and (4) of the Rules of Procedure, the Committee on Legal Affairs had, at its meeting of 24 May 2011, verified the credentials and unanimously proposed the validation of the mandate of the MEPs Hubert Pirker with effect from 31 March 2011, Paul Murphy and Heinz K. Becker with effect from 1 April 2011, Mojca Kleva with effect from 13 April 2011, Phil Prendergast with effect from 21 April 2011 and Sampo Terho with effect from 26 April 2011.


6. Composition of political groups

David Campbell Bannerman had joined the ECR Group with effect from 24 May 2011.

Theodoros Skylakakis had joined the ALDE Group with effect from 31 May 2011.


7. Signature of acts adopted under the ordinary legislative procedure

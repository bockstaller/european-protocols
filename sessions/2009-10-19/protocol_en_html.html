IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Statement by the President

The President made a statement in which he welcomed the signing of the act of ratification of the Lisbon Treaty by the President of Poland, Lech Kaczyński.

°
° ° °

The President stated that a 'Question Hour' with the President of the Commission would be held on Tuesday at 15.00.

°
° ° °

The President made a statement on the occasion of the International Day for the Abolition of the Death Penalty, and called on all countries applying the death penalty to abolish it and to impose a moratorium on executions.

4. Action taken on Parliament's resolutions

The Commission communication on the action it had taken in response to the resolutions adopted by Parliament during the March I and March II 2009 part-sessions was available on the Séance en direct site.


5. Composition of Parliament

Athanasios Pafilis and Athanasios Plevris had informed the President that they had been elected to the Greek Parliament.

Parliament noted this and, under Article 7(2) of the Act concerning the election of members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage and under Rule 4(4) of the Rules of Procedure, established the vacancies with effect from 14 October 2009.

The Greek authorities had announced the following elections to the European Parliament:

- Spyridon Danellis, to replace Giorgos Papakonstantinou, with effect from 8 October 2009,
- Charalampos Aggourakis, to replace Athanasios Pafilis, with effect from 14 October 2009,
- Nikolaos Salavrakos, to replace Athanasios Plevris, with effect from 14 October 2009.

Under Rule 4(1), Parliament noted these appointments. In accordance with Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Giorgos Papakonstantinou, Athanasios Pafilis and Athanasios Plevris would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

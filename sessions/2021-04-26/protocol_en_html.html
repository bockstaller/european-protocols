1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday 25 March 2021 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.00.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 24 March 2021 and 25 March 2021 were approved.


4. Request for waiver of immunity

The Belgian authorities had forwarded a request to waive the immunity of Fulvio Martusciello in order to bring him before a police court.

Pursuant to Rule 9(1), the request had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.


5. Assessment of compatibility of a mission entrusted to an MEP

At its meeting of 22 April 2021, the Committee on Legal Affairs had undertaken, pursuant to Rule 4(5), an assessment of the compliance with the Act of 20 September 1976 of the temporary mission on Europe and the French education system entrusted by the Prime Minister of France to Ilana Cicurel, and found it to be compatible with the Act.


6. Extension of the mandate of the Committee of Inquiry on the Protection of Animals during transport

The President announced that during its meeting on 22 April 2021, the Conference of Presidents had approved the request of the Committee of Inquiry on the Protection of Animals during Transport to extend its mandate again by three months.

That new extension was necessary to allow the committee of inquiry to fully and correctly fulfil its mandate as adopted by Parliament on 19 June 2020 (minutes of 19.6.2020, item 6).

If no objection was raised in the 24 hours following that announcement, that extension would be deemed to be approved. In the event of such an objection, it would be put to a vote.


7. Composition of Parliament

Derk Jan Eppink and Kati Piri had been elected members of the national parliament of the Netherlands with effect from 31 March 2021.

Parliament took note that, in accordance with Article 7(2) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, that office was incompatible with the office of Member of the European Parliament and declared their seats vacant as from 31 March 2021 pursuant to Rule 4(1) and (4).

⁂

The competent authorities of the Netherlands had notified the President of the European Parliament that Michiel Hoogeveen and Thijs Reuten had been elected as Members of the European Parliament to replace Derk Jan Eppink and Kati Piri, with effect from 15 April 2021.

Parliament took due note of their election.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Michiel Hoogeveen and Thijs Reuten would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

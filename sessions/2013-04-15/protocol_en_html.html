7. Announcement by the President

The President said that Nikiforos Diamandouros had announced he would be stepping down as European Ombudsman with effect from 1 October 2013. A call for applications had been published in the Official Journal on 4 April; the closing date for forwarding applications to the President was 8 May 2013.

Under Rule 204(2), each application had to have the support of at least 40 Members who were nationals of at least two Member States, and Members could support only one application. If a Member supported several applications, none of these choices would be taken into account. Once an application had been forwarded to the President, i.e. after 8 May, it would no longer be possible to withdraw or add a supporting signature.

Under Rule 204(3), applications that met all the criteria would be forwarded to the Chair of the Committee on Petitions. The Committee on Petitions would hold hearings of applicants, probably in mid-June. The hearings would be open to all Members.

The President added that the election in plenary would be held during the July part-session so the new Ombudsman could take up his duties on 1 October 2013.

This information would be forwarded to all Members in the form of a communication from the President sent from the 'Ombudsman Election 2013' electronic mailbox which had been set up for this purpose and to receive Members' questions on this subject.

9. Interpretation of the Rules of Procedure

The President informed the House, under Rule 211(3), of the following interpretation of Rule 159(3), provided by the Committee on Constitutional Affairs, which had been consulted on the application of that provision:

Rule 159(3) is to be interpreted as meaning that, where there is a tied vote on a draft recommendation under Rule 128(4) not to intervene in proceedings before the Court of Justice of the European Union, such a tie does not signify the adoption of a recommendation that Parliament should intervene in those proceedings. In such a case, the committee responsible is to be deemed not to have expressed any recommendation.

If this interpretation was not contested by a political group or by at least 40 Members, in accordance with Rule 211(4), up until the opening of the following day's sitting, Tuesday 16 April 2013, it would be deemed to have been adopted. Otherwise, the matter would be put to the vote in Parliament.

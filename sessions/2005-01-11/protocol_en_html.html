6.2.  Election of the European Ombudsman

Election of the European Ombudsman

(Secret ballot)

The President stated that the following nominations had been received:

- Nikiforos Diamandouros

- Giuseppe Fortunato

(Two other nominations had been declared inadmissible.)

The President reminded the House of the provisions of Rule 194(5) and announced that an electronic vote would take place.

He noted that as more than 50 per cent of Members were present, the election could take place.

Members voting:      643

Blank or void ballot papers:    34

Votes cast:         609

Majority required:      305

(The list of Members voting appears in the 'Result of roll-call votes' document)

The results were as follows:

Nikiforos Diamandouros :   564 votes

Giuseppe Fortunato :       45 votes

Having obtained a majority of the votes cast, Nikiforos Diamandouros was elected European Ombudsman.

(P6_TA(2005)0002 )

The President congratulated him on his election and invited him to swear an oath in due course before the Court of Justice, in accordance with the Rules of Procedure.

Margot Wallström (Vice-President of the Commission) also expressed her congratulations.

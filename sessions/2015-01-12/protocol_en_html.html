IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the terrorist attacks in Paris the previous week. He read out the names of the seventeen people who had been killed in these attacks. He strongly condemned acts such acts against our freedoms and values and sent Parliament's condolences to the victims' families.

He also paid tribute to the victims of the latest murderous attack by Boko Haram in Nigeria.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.

The following spoke: Alain Lamassoure, on behalf of the PPE Group, Pervenche Berès, on behalf of the S&D Group, Helga Stevens, on behalf of the ECR Group, Guy Verhofstadt, on behalf of the ALDE Group, Patrick Le Hyaric, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Michèle Rivasi, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Nigel Farage, on behalf of the EFDD Group, and Marine Le Pen, Non-attached Member.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


4. Composition of Parliament

Following the death of Philip Bradbourn, Parliament established, under Rule 4(1), that his seat had been vacant since 21 December 2014.

°
° ° °

The Belgian authorities had announced the election to the European Parliament of Hilde Vautmans to replace Annemie Neyts-Uyttebroeck and of Anneleen Van Bossuyt to replace Louis Ide as Members of the European Parliament with effect from 1 January 2015 and 8 January 2015 respectively.

In accordance with Article 7(1) of the Act of 20 September 1976 concerning the election of the Members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage and Rule 4(1), Parliament took note of the election of Hilde Vautmans with effect from 1 January 2015 and of Anneleen Van Bossuyt with effect from 8 January 2015.

In accordance with Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Hilde Vautmans and Anneleen Van Bossuyt would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.


5. Composition of committees and delegations

The President had received the following requests for appointments from the ALDE and ECR Groups:

AFET Committee: Hilde Vautmans

Subcommittee on Security and Defence: Hilde Vautmans

Delegation for relations with the NATO Parliamentary Assembly: Hilde Vautmans

Delegation to the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly: Ian Duncan to replace Andrew Lewer.

The appointments would be deemed ratified if there were no objections before the approval of these minutes.


6. Signature of acts adopted under the ordinary legislative procedure (Rule 78)

The President announced that since the last part-session he and the President of the Council had together signed the following acts adopted in accordance with the ordinary legislative procedure:

- Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council on the tariff treatment for goods originating in Ecuador (00096/2014/LEX - C8-0297/2014 - 2014/0287(COD))

- Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council amending Council Regulation (EC) No 55/2008 introducing autonomous trade preferences for the Republic of Moldova (00095/2014/LEX - C8-0296/2014 - 2014/0250(COD))

8. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the January I 2015 sittings (PE 546.150/PDOJ) had been distributed and a number of changes had been proposed (Rule 152):

No changes.

Wednesday

Request by the Verts/ALE to enter on Wednesday's agenda a statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the recent attacks by Boko Haram in the north of Nigeria, with the tabling of motions for resolutions.

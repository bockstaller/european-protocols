5. Tribute

On behalf of Parliament, the President paid tribute to the memory of Jens Nilsson, Member of the European Parliament, who had died the previous day.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.

The following spoke: Maria João Rodrigues, on behalf of the S&D Group.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: Ashley Fox, on the attempted assassination in Salisbury (United Kingdom) of a Russian national and his daughter by means of a toxic agent, Steven Woolfe, on a personal matter in response to what Alyn Smith had said that morning during the debate on the Guidelines on the future EU-UK relations (minutes of 13.3.2018, item 3), Alyn Smith, who explained but refused to withdraw what he had said, William (The Earl of) Dartmouth, who called for Alyn Smith to be reprimanded (the President said he considered the explanation Alyn Smith had given to be sufficient), Lynn Boylan, who announced that Ibrahim Halawa (an Irish-Egyptian citizen imprisoned in Egypt from 2013 to 2017 and whose release Parliament had demanded) was present in the visitors' gallery, and David Coburn.


6. Composition of Parliament

Following the death of Jens Nilsson, Parliament established the vacancy of his seat with effect from 13 March 2018, pursuant to Rule 4(1) and (6).

12. Request for consultation of the European Economic and Social Committee (Rule 137(2))

The President announced that no request had been received from a political group or Members reaching at least the low threshold to put to a vote the request for consultation of the European Economic and Social Committee with a view to drawing up an own-initiative report on the situation and rights of disabled women announced in the minutes of Monday 12 March 2018 (minutes of 12.3.2018, item 8).

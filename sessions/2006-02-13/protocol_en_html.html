9.  Order of business

The next item was the order of business.

The final draft agenda for the February part-session (PE 368.298/PDOJ) had been distributed. The following changes had been proposed under Rule 132:

Sittings of 13.02.2006 to 16.02.2006

Monday

- no changes

Tuesday

- no changes

Wednesday

The ALDE Group had submitted three requests after the deadline laid down in Rule 132(1). However, having established that there were no objections, the President decided to put the requests to the House:

- request from the ALDE Group for more than one speaker per group to be allowed to speak during the debate on the Council and Commission statements on the right to freedom of expression and respect for religious beliefs (Item 55 on the final draft agenda)

- request from the ALDE Group for the debate to be wound up with motions for resolutions

- request from the ALDE Group for the Council and Commission statements on the outlook for Bosnia-Herzegovina (Item 30 on the final draft agenda) to be held over until a later part-session

The following spoke: Annemie Neyts-Uyttebroeck , on behalf of the ALDE Group , who moved the requests.

The following spoke on the requests: Daniel Cohn-Bendit , on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group , who called for a block vote on the three requests, Martin Schulz , on behalf of the PSE Group, Hans-Gert Poettering , on behalf of the PPE-DE Group , who was not in favour of a block vote, and Daniel Cohn-Bendit .

Vote on the ALDE Group's first request:

Parliament rejected the request.

Vote on the ALDE Group's second request:

Parliament approved the request by electronic vote (108 in favour, 72 against, 15 abstentions).

Hannes Swoboda , speaking on behalf of the PSE Group, asked that the vote on the motions for resolutions be included on the agenda for the following part-session.

The following spoke on the request: Monica Frassoni , Johannes Voggenhuber , Hans-Gert Poettering , Daniel Cohn-Bendit , Martin Schulz , Bernd Posselt and Johannes Voggenhuber .

Parliament rejected Hannes Swoboda 's request by electronic vote (99 in favour , 113 against , 9 abstentions ). The vote would thus be taken during that part-session.

Deadlines for tabling amendments and motions for resolutions:

- motions for resolution: Tuesday, 14.02.2006, 10.00

- joint motions for resolutions and amendments: Wednesday, 15.02.2006, 10.00

Vote: Thursday 16.02.2006.

Vote on the ALDE Group's third request:

The following spoke: José Ignacio Salafranca Sánchez-Neyra , who took the view that the request should not be put to the vote. The President agreed.

Thursday

- no changes

The order of business was thus established.

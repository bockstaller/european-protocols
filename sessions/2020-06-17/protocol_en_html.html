IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Wednesday 27 May 2020 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 15.02.


3. Statement by the President

The President offered his heartfelt condolences and those of Parliament to the family and friends of George Floyd, who died in the United States on 25 May 2020.

The President noted that Parliament had always strongly condemned all kinds of violence and that he supported the rule of law.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of George Floyd and all victims of violence, racism and discrimination.


4. Announcement by the President

The President recalled the preventive measures introduced at Parliament to enable social distancing during that part-session.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: Manon Aubry on the recent anti-racism demonstrations, Angel Dzhambazki on the allocation of speaking time, Pierrette Herzberger-Fofana to announce that she had been assaulted by the Belgian police the previous day and to ask for specific action to be taken (the President took note of this), and Philippe Lamberts on the speech by Pierrette Herzberger-Fofana.


5. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

Gilles Lebreton had made it known that he had been present but that his name was not on the attendance register.


6. Composition of Parliament

Klaus Buchner had given notice in writing of his resignation as Member of Parliament, with effect from 16 July 2020.

In accordance with Rule 4, Parliament established the vacancy from that date and informed the national authority concerned.


7. Composition of committees and delegations

The President had received from the Verts/ALE Group the following decision changing the composition of the committees and delegations:

Delegation to the Euronest Parliamentary Assembly: Heidi Hautala

The decision took effect as of that day.


8. Calendar of part-sessions

The President announced that, having regard to COVID-19 and with a view to enabling Parliament to exercise its core functions, he had decided, together with the political group leaders, at the last meeting of the Conference of Presidents on Thursday, 28 May 2020:

—   to prolong the current part-session until Friday, 19 June 2020,

—   and that July's part-session would take place in Brussels from the morning of Wednesday, 8 July 2020 to the afternoon of Friday, 10 July 2020.

The revised calendar was available on Parliament's website.

IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended Thursday 14 November 2019 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.06.


3. Statement by the President

The President made a statement to mark that year's International Day for the Elimination of Violence against Women. He called for joint action to ensure gender equality, prevent violence against women and protect victims. He further urged all EU Member States to ratify the Istanbul Convention as a matter of urgency.

The President announced that Parliament was participating in the 'Orange the World' initiative and that the Parliament building would be bathed in orange light that evening to express solidarity with all victims of violence against women.

Parliament observed a minute's silence to commemorate all of these victims.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

5. Announcements by the President

The President announced that, pursuant to Rule 176(1), he had decided to censure Manon Aubry for appealing to 'Extinction Rebellion' activists to occupy the European Parliament in a video recorded on Parliament's premises and published on her YouTube and Twitter accounts on 17 October 2019. This behaviour constituted a serious breach of Rule 10(2).

Ms Aubry was issued a reprimand.

The President had also decided, again pursuant to Rule 176(1), to censure Pierre Larrouturou for the behaviour of certain individuals he had helped enter Parliament's buildings on 16 October 2019 and whom he was obliged to accompany. This behaviour constituted a serious breach of Rules 10(2) and (7).

The President reminded Members of their responsibility concerning the behaviour of visitors they welcome to Parliament.

Mr Larrouturou was issued a reprimand.

Both Members had been informed, and were entitled to lodge an internal appeal with the Bureau pursuant to Rule 177.

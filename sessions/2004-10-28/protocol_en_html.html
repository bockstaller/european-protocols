1.  Opening of sitting

The sitting opened at 10.05.

The following spoke:

- Giles Chichester (chairman of the ITRE Committee), who sought confirmation that all the letters of assessment on the Commissioners-designate had been forwarded to José Manuel Barroso , President-elect of the Commission (the President replied that his question would be forwarded to the relevant authorities);

- Miguel Angel Martínez Martínez , who asked that Loyola de Palacio (Vice-President of the Commission) correct certain remarks she had recently made in the Spanish press that he believed were insulting to several Member States. He thought that Romano Prodi , acting President of the Commission, should intervene in the matter (the President replied that he thought the matter would be dealt with appropriately).

Details of voting (amendments, separate and split votes, etc.) appear in Annex 1 to the Minutes.


4.1.  Draft general budget of the European Union – 2005 financial year (vote)

- draft amendments and proposed modifications to the appropriations in Section III of the draft general budget for 2005 relating to the Commission
- draft amendments to Sections I, II, IV, V, VI, VII and VIII of the draft general budget for 2005 relating to the European Parliament, the Council, the Court of Justice, the Court of Auditors, the Economic and Social Committee, the Committee of the Regions, the European Ombudsman and the European Data Protection Supervisor

Under the Treaties, draft amendments (covering non-compulsory expenditure) require for adoption the votes of a majority of Parliament's component Members. Proposed modifications (covering compulsory expenditure) require only a majority of the votes cast.

(Voting record: Annex 1, Item 1)

Draft amendments and proposed modifications are listed in the annex to the 'Texts Adopted'.

The following spoke before the vote:

- Salvador Garriga Polledo (general rapporteur) to introduce technical corrections to amendments 592, 65, 66, 67, 69 and 599, as well as to budget line 16 05 01;

- Lars Wohlin , on behalf of the IND/DEM Group , to suggest that the corrections introduced by the general rapporteur be put to the vote by roll call (noting that Lars Wohlin did not insist that his request be acted upon, the President did not take up the suggestion).

Parliament approved the technical corrections introduced by the general rapporteur.

The following spoke during the vote:

- Salvador Garriga Polledo on amendment 586;

- Anne Elisabet Jensen (rapporteur on sections other than section III), to clarify amendments 436 and 370 (budget line 209);

- Salvador Garriga Polledo on amendments 495, 68 and 343; after the vote on block 7, he pointed out that the ceiling in heading 3 had been exceeded; he returned to this point at the end of the vote, saying he would call a coordinators' meeting in an attempt to find a solution;

- Salvador Garriga Polledo , Markus Ferber and Dirk Sterckx on the majority required to adopt the 'remarks' pertaining to amendments 610 and 210;

- Salvador Garriga Polledo on block 16.

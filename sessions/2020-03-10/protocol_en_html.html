IN THE CHAIR: Mairead McGUINNESS
Vice-President

1. Opening of the session

In accordance with Article 229, first paragraph, of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and with Rule 154, the 2020-2021 session of the European Parliament opened.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 9.02.


3. Statement by the President

The President made a statement to mark International Women's Day 2020.

She highlighted the efforts that women were making in the difficult circumstances brought about by the coronavirus. She also alluded to the gender equality strategy that the Commission had recently brought forward, and pointed out that Parliament was totally committed to strengthening gender equality and women's rights in the European Union and across the world.

The following spoke: Frances Fitzgerald, on behalf of the PPE Group, Iratxe García Pérez, on behalf of the S&D Group, Irène Tolleret, on behalf of the Renew Group, Catherine Griset, on behalf of the ID Group, Ska Keller, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Lucia Ďuriš Nicholsonová, on behalf of the ECR Group, and Manon Aubry, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group.

The following spoke: Nikolina Brnjac (President-in-Office of the Council).

9. Calendar of part-sessions

Given the situation with regard to the coronavirus, to guarantee security and public health protection and to enable Parliament to carry out its core activities, the Conference of Presidents had, at its meeting that day, taken the following decision:

the April I 2020 part-session would be held in Brussels on 1 and 2 April 2020.

The revised calendar was available on Parliament's website.


10. Request for waiver of immunity

The Portuguese authorities had forwarded a request for the waiver of Nuno Melo's immunity to allow for a court case in which he was an applicant to continue.

Pursuant to Rule 9(1), the request had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.

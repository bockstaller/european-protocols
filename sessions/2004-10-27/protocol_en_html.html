5.  Voting time

Election of the European Commission (vote)

Motions for resolution B6-0088/2004 , B6-0090/2004 , B6-0092/2004 , B6-0093/2004 , B6-0098/2004 , B6-0099/2004 and B6-0100/2004 and draft decision B6-0091/2004

José Manuel Barroso (President-elect of the Commission) stated that, in view of the situation, he had decided not to submit a proposal on a new Commission for Parliament's approval that day.

The President noted the decision and, having reminded the House of the provisions of Article 214 of the EC Treaty, pointed out that as no proposal had been made, Parliament could not vote on the matter.

Atzo Nicolaï (President-in-Office of the Council) stated that, under the circumstances, the incumbent Commission would stay in office for as long as necessary.

Marco Pannella spoke on Article 214 of the EC Treaty and Rules 99(2) and 132 of the Rules of Procedure (the President cut him off, as his remarks did not constitute a point of order under the Rules).

The following spoke: Martin Schulz , on behalf of the PSE Group , who asked that the sitting be suspended to give the political groups time to meet; and Hans-Gert Poettering , on behalf of the PPE-DE Group , who echoed the request.

The President stated that he would call a meeting of the Conference of Presidents after the political group meetings.

(The sitting was suspended at 11.15 and resumed at 12.15.)

Bogusław Rogalski spoke on a procedural matter.

The following spoke: Hans-Gert Poettering , on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, Martin Schulz , on behalf of the PSE Group, Graham Watson , on behalf of the ALDE Group, Daniel Marc Cohn-Bendit , on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Francis Wurtz , on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Jens-Peter Bonde , on behalf of the IND/DEM Group, Cristiana Muscardini , on behalf of the UEN Group, and Ryszard Czarnecki , Non-attached Member .

The following spoke on procedural matters: Robert Kilroy-Silk (the President cut him off, as his remarks did not constitute a point of order under the Rules) and Alessandra Mussolini .

The following spoke: Atzo Nicolaï and José Manuel Barroso .

In the light of the remarks made by the previous speakers, the President concluded firstly that, as no proposal had been made, there would be no vote; and secondly that the incumbent Commission would remain in office. He asked José Manuel Barroso and the Council to submit a proposal as soon as possible and to keep Parliament informed of the state of play.

(The sitting was suspended at 13.05 and resumed at 15.05.)

10.  Question Time (Council)

Question 4 (Jonas Sjöstedt): Relations with Turkey  (H-0312/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the questions and supplementaries by Dimitrios Papadimoulis , Jonas Sjöstedt and Antonios Trakatellis .

Question 6 (Jillian Evans): Flooding in Bangladesh  (H-0279/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and a supplementary by Robert Evans .

Question 7 (Bernd Posselt): Border controls in south-eastern Europe  (H-0280/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and a supplementary by Bernd Posselt .

Question 8 (Jacek Protasiewicz): The accession of Turkey to the European Union  (H-0281/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and supplementaries by Jacek Protasiewicz , Robert Evans and Bernd Posselt .

Question 9 (Georgios Karatzaferis): Provocative statements by the Albanian Prime Minister  (H-0284/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question.

Question 10 (Manuel Medina Ortega): Beaked whale deaths following military manoeuvres in the Atlantic  (H-0289/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and a supplementary by Manuel Medina Ortega .

Question 11 (Frederika Brepoels): European Council's position on the possible scrapping of euro coins  (H-0296/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and a supplementary by Frederika Brepoels .

Question 12 (Brian Crowley): Cooperation between police agencies and coastguards in preventing illegal drug imports  (H-0304/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and supplementaries by Brian Crowley , Claude Moraes and Bill Newton Dunn .

Question 13 (Eoin Ryan): Third world countries and the debt burden  (H-0306/04 ) .

Atzo Nicolaï answered the question and a supplementary by Eoin Ryan .

The following spoke: Bill Newton Dunn and Manolis Mavrommatis on the conduct of Question Time.

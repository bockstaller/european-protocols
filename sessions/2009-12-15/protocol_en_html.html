2. Announcement by the President

The President announced that on 2 December 2009 Parliament had received a Communication from the Commission to the European Parliament and the Council entitled 'Consequences of the entry into force of the Treaty of Lisbon for ongoing interinstitutional decision-making procedures' (COM(2009)0665 final).

He stated that Parliament as an institution deemed, by virtue of that communication, that all the proposals listed therein for which the Treaty of Lisbon granted it competence had been resubmitted to Parliament, and that it had also taken note of the fact that, by way of that communication, the Commission had withdrawn certain proposals.

In the case of legislative proposals pending as at 1 December 2009, the date on which the Treaty of Lisbon had entered into force, the President invited the parliamentary committees to which proposals had already been submitted in the current legislative term, as recorded in the minutes of the part-sessions, to verify the legal bases and procedural modifications proposed by the Commission, along with any other amendments to be made in the light of the provisions of the Treaty of Lisbon, and to decide whether they intended to adjudge positions already adopted under the consultation procedure to be ordinary legislative procedure first readings or to constitute approvals, with it being understood that Parliament reserved the right, in all cases, to request that the Commission submit a new proposal.

In the case of proposals on which Parliament had not been consulted prior to entry into force of the Lisbon Treaty, it fell to the Commission to forward new proposals under the appropriate procedure.

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended Tuesday 10 March 2020 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 10.00.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


4. Calendar of part-sessions

The President announced that, at his suggestion and given the severity of the situation and the significant increase of cases of COVID 19 in the EU, at its meeting of Thursday 19 March, the Conference of Presidents had decided to invoke force majeure and, for reasons of public health and safety, make further amendments to Parliament's calendar of activities for 2020.

Since this extraordinary part-session was being held today, Thursday 26 March, the Brussels part-sessions that had been scheduled for 1-2 April, 22-23 April and 3-4 June were cancelled.

The part-session originally scheduled for 11-14 May in Strasbourg would be held on 13-14 May in Brussels;

the part-session originally scheduled for 15-18 June in Strasbourg would be held on 17-18 June in Brussels;

the part-session originally scheduled for 6-9 July in Strasbourg would be held on 8-9 July in Brussels.

The Conference of Presidents would reassess the situation in the light of developments in the COVID-19 pandemic.

The revised provisional calendar was available on Parliament's website.

18. Statement by the President

The President made a statement outlining the exceptional and unforeseen circumstances that had caused the extraordinary part-session to be convened. The purpose was to protect the health of Parliament's staff and Members, while ensuring that Parliament continued to constitute a beacon of democracy in the service of the EU's people.

He expressed Parliament's condolences to the family of the young man working at Parliament who had died of COVID-19, and to the families of all those who had lost their lives as a result of the virus.

He paid tribute to the dedication of the EU's medical staff and pointed out that the manner in which people behaved would be decisive in the fight against the pandemic.

He concluded by stressing the importance of the decisions to be taken that day.

28. Explanations of vote

Written explanations of vote:

Explanations of vote submitted in writing under Rule 194 appear on the Members' pages on Parliament's website.

Exceptionally, explanations of vote of up to 400 words would be accepted.


29. Corrections to votes and voting intentions

Corrections to votes and voting intentions could be found on Parliament's website under 'Results of roll-call votes' as an annex to the minutes of the sitting. They are for information only and have no effect on the result of the vote as announced in plenary.

The document will be regularly updated in the two weeks following the part-session concerned.

After those two weeks, the list of corrections to votes and voting intentions will be finalised so that it can be translated and published in the Official Journal.

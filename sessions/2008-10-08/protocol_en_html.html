4.  Composition of Parliament

Joseph Muscat had given notice of his election to the Maltese Parliament.

Under Article 7(2) of the Act concerning the election of representatives of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage and Rule 4(1) of its Rules of Procedure, Parliament noted this and established the vacancy with effect from 01.10.2008.

°
° ° °

The French authorities had given notice of:

- the election of Bernadette Bourzai and Robert Navarro to the French Senate, and

- the election to the European Parliament of Jean-Paul Denanot , replacing Bernadette Bourzai , and of Michel Teychenne , replacing Robert Navarro .

Under Article 7(2) of the Act concerning the election of representatives of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage and Rule 4(1) of its Rules of Procedure, Parliament established the vacancy of the seats of Bernadette Bourzai and Robert Navarro with effect from 01.10.2008, and noted the election to Parliament of Jean-Paul Denanot and Michel Teychenne with effect from 06.10.2008.

°
° ° °

The UK authorities had given notice of the election to the European Parliament of Trevor Colman , in place of Graham Booth , with effect from 01.10.2008.

°
° ° °

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made written declarations stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Jean-Paul Denanot, Michel Teychenne and Trevor Colman would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

11.  Order of business

The next item was the order of business.

The final draft agenda for the October I plenary sittings (PE 413.272) had been distributed. The President, with the agreement of the political groups, proposed the following amendments (Rule 132):

Sittings of 08.10.2008 and 09.10.2008

1. A debate on the situation of the global financial system would be included in the debate on the preparation of the European Council (item 3 on the final draft agenda ), the title of which would be changed accordingly. The debate would be wound up with the tabling of a motion for a resolution by the ECON Committee, which would be put to the vote during the October II part-session in Strasbourg.

2. The debate on the report by Ieke van den Burg and Daniel Dăianu entitled 'Lamfalussy follow up - Future structure of supervision' (A6-0359/2008 ) (item 16 on the final draft agenda ) would be brought forward from the agenda of the following day, Thursday 09.10.2008, to Wednesday's, and placed after the debate on the preparation of the European Council, including the situation of the global financial system.

3. The debate on the report by Carlos Carnero González on the use by Parliament of the symbols of the Union (A6-0347/2008 ) (item 7 on the final draft agenda ) would be the first item on the following day's agenda.

4. The report by Jo Leinen on the approval of the joint declaration on Communicating Europe in Partnership (A6-0372/2008 ), adopted by the AFCO Committee on 07.10.2008, would be put to the vote during the following day's voting time in accordance with Rule 43(1).

5. Wednesday's business would continue uninterrupted until 24.00.

The President noted that there were no objections to the proposed amendments.

The order of business was thus established.

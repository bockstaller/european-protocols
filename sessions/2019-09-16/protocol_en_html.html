1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday, 18 July 2019 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.11.


3. Tribute

On behalf of Parliament, the President paid tribute to the memory of André Jorge Dionísio Bradford MEP, who had died suddenly.


4. Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the recent floods in Spain, which had caused considerable damage and as a result of which several lives had been lost.

The President expressed Parliament's solidarity with the families of the victims and paid tribute to the work done by the emergency services.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in honour of the victims of the floods and in memory of André Jorge Dionísio Bradford.

6. Composition of Parliament

Following the death of André Jorge Dionísio Bradford, Parliament established that his seat was vacant with effect from 19 July 2019, in accordance with Rule 4(1) and (6).

Roberto Gualtieri had been appointed Minister of the Economy and Finance by the Italian Government with effect from 5 September 2019.

Parliament noted that, pursuant to Article 7(1) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, this office was incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament and, pursuant to Rule 4(1) and (4) of its Rules of Procedure, established that his seat was vacant with effect from 5 September 2019.

°
° ° °

The Danish authorities had given notice of the election of Marianne Vind to replace Jeppe Kofod as Member of Parliament with effect from 2 July 2019.

The Portuguese authorities had given notice of the election of Isabel Carvalhais to replace André Jorge Dionísio Bradford as Member of Parliament with effect from 3 September 2019.

Parliament took note of their election.

In accordance with Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, Marianne Vind and Isabel Carvalhais would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto, provided that they had previously signed a written declaration that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament.

11. Announcement by the President – Election of the European Ombudsman

The President informed Members that a call for applications with a view to the election of the European Ombudsman had been published in the Official Journal on 30 August 2019, with the closing date for submitting applications to the President being 30 September 2019.

The President stated that, under Rule 231(2), each application had to have the support of at least 40 Members who were nationals of at least two Member States, and that Members could support only one application. If a Member supported several applications, none of those choices would be taken into account. Once an application had been submitted to the President, it would no longer be possible to withdraw or add a supporting signature.

Applications that met all the criteria would be forwarded to the Chair of the Committee on Petitions. The Committee would hold hearings of applicants in December. The hearings would be open to all Members.

The President also announced that the election was due to take place during the December 2019 part-session.

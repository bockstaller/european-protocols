IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Announcement by the President

The President outlined the incident involving Steven Woolfe and another Member from his political group that that had taken place on Thursday, 6 October 2016 on Parliament premises. The President recalled the provisions of Rule 11(2) of the Rules of Procedure and announced that the matter had been referred to the Advisory Committee on the Conduct of Members, which would inform Parliament of the results of its deliberations.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

Krišjānis Kariņš stated that he had wished to abstain in the vote of 15 September 2016 on the report by Laura Agea on the Proposal for a Council decision on guidelines for the employment policies of the Member States (A8-0247/2016) (item 11.8 of minutes of 15.09.2016).


4. Composition of Parliament

The Spanish authorities had given notice of the election of Florent Marcellesi to replace Jordi Sebastià as Member of Parliament with effect from 11 October 2016.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as his credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that he had previously made a written declaration stating that he did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Florent Marcellesi would take his seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

°
° ° °

Andrej Plenković and Davor Ivo Stier had given notice in writing of their resignations as Members of Parliament, with effect from 14 October 2016.

Under Rule 4(1) and (3) Parliament established that their seats were vacant from that date and informed the national authorities concerned.

Matthias Groote and Richard Howitt had also given notice in writing of their resignations as Members of Parliament, with effect from 1 and 2 November 2016, respectively.

Under Rule 4(1) and (3) Parliament established that their seats were vacant from that date and informed the national authorities concerned.


5. Request for the waiver of parliamentary immunity

The Portuguese authorities had forwarded a request to waive the immunity of António Marinho e Pinto in relation to criminal proceedings brought against him by the Public Prosecutor's Office for the Lisbon West district.

The French authorities had forwarded a request to waive the immunity of Marine Le Pen in relation to a judicial inquiry opened at Nanterre Regional Court.

Pursuant to Rule 9(1), the requests had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.


6. Composition of political groups

Steven Woolfe was no longer a member of the EFDD Group, and as from 24 October 2016 would sit as a non-attached Member.

8. Delegated acts (Rule 105(6))

In accordance with Rule 105(6), the Chair of the Conference of Committee Chairs had informed the President of Parliament that no objections had been raised with regard to:

- Commission delegated regulation of 4 October 2016 supplementing Regulation (EU) No 648/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council on OTC derivatives, central counterparties and trade repositories with regard to regulatory technical standards for risk-mitigation techniques for OTC derivative contracts not cleared by a central counterparty (C(2016)06329 – 2016/2930(DEA))(B8-1124/2016)

If no objections to this recommendation are raised by a political group or at least 40 Members within 24 hours, it shall be deemed to have been approved. If this is not the case, it shall be put to the vote.

The recommendation is available on Europarl for the duration of the current part-session.

IN THE CHAIR: Hans-Gert PÖTTERING
President

1.  Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 9.00.


2.  Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the occasion of the Fifth European Day for the Victims of Terrorism. He paid homage to all victims of terrorism, referring in particular to the two British soldiers killed on 7 March 2009 in Massereene and the policeman killed on 9 March 2009 in County Armagh, Northern Ireland, as well as the victims of the suicide attack carried out on 10 March 2009 against a mosque in southern Sri Lanka. He condemned terrorist acts as one of the greatest dangers to the security, stability and democratic values of the international community. He expressed, on behalf of Parliament, his solidarity with the families of the victims.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of the victims of the recent attacks in Northern Ireland and Sri Lanka.

°
° ° °

The President made a statement on the occasion of the 30th anniversary of the death of Jean Monnet. He paid homage to his memory and to the principles, which still applied today, that had guided his actions.

The following spoke: José Manuel Barroso (President of the Commission) , who associated himself with the homage paid by the President.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: José Ribeiro e Castro , who said that the European Day for the Victims of Terrorism ought to be more widely celebrated throughout the Union, and called on the Commission and the Council Presidency to take initiatives to that effect.

The President said that Robert Evans and forty other Members had presented an objection in writing to the inclusion on the agenda of the motion for a resolution by the AFET Committee on the humanitarian situation in Sri Lanka (item 14 of the minutes of 10.03.2009) .

8.  Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the tragic events that had taken place that day at a school in Winnenden , in the German state of Baden-W ü rttemberg , where a former pupil had killed several schoolchildren and teachers and wounded two policemen. The President also spoke of similar events that had occurred six months previously at a school in Kauhajoki , Finland, and the previous day in Alabama, United States.

He called on the European Union and its Member States to do everything in their power to prevent such acts and, on behalf of Parliament, expressed his solidarity with the families of the victims.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of the victims.

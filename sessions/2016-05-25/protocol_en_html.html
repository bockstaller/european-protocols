IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 15.05.


2. Tribute

The President paid tribute, on behalf of Parliament, to the memory of Marco Pannella, a former Member.

(The sitting was suspended for a short time, pending the start of the formal sitting.)


3. Formal sitting - Netherlands

From 15.10 to 15.30, a formal sitting of Parliament was held on the occasion of the visit of His Majesty Willem-Alexander, King of the Netherlands.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


5. Motion of censure on the Commission

The President drew attention to his statement of 18 May 2016 that some Members had withdrawn their signatures from the motion of censure on the Commission which had been announced on 12 May 2016 (minutes of 12.5.2016, item 14).

As the motion of censure no longer bore the signatures of one tenth of the component Members of Parliament, it lapsed and the procedure was closed.

The following spoke: Jean-Luc Schaffhauser on the procedure followed (the President explained the rules).


6. Composition of Parliament

Peter Eriksson had been appointed a member of the Swedish Government.

Parliament noted that, pursuant to Article 7(1) of the Act of 20 September 1976 concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, this post was incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament. In accordance with Rule 3(2) and Rule 4(1), Parliament established the vacancy of his seat with effect from 25 May 2016, and the competent national authorities would be informed.


7. Verification of credentials

On a proposal from the JURI Committee, Parliament confirmed the validity of the mandate of Lieve Wierinck with effect from 4 May 2016.

13. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the May II 2016 sittings (PE 583.362/PDOJ) had been distributed and the following changes had been proposed (Rule 152):

Wednesday

—   The President proposed, after consultation with the political groups, to enter on the agenda a statement by the Commission on 'Endocrine disruptors: state of play following the Court judgment of 16 December 2015', after the Council and Commission statements on 'Transatlantic data flows'.

   Parliament approved the proposal.

   Accordingly, the sitting would be extended to midnight.

—   Request from the Verts/ALE Group to wind up that debate with the tabling of motions for resolutions which would be put to the vote at the June I part-session.

   The following spoke: Philippe Lamberts, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, to move the request, and Kathleen Van Brempt, in favour of the request.

   Parliament approved the request.

—   Request from the ENF Group to enter as the first item on the agenda after the formal sitting a statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on 'The situation following the elections in Congo-Brazzaville'.

   The following spoke: Louis Aliot, on behalf of the ENF Group, to move the request, and Mario Borghezio in favour of the request.

   Parliament rejected the request.

—   The President announced that, in accordance with Rule 113(4), 40 Members had submitted a written objection to the procedure employed for the report by Andrey Kovatchev - A8-0146/2016 - on 'The 71st session of the UN General Assembly', and that the report would be entered on the agenda of a subsequent part-session.

Thursday

—   The President announced that, following the approval by the Committee on Legal Affairs of the report by Evelyn Regner - A8-0180/2016 - on 'The request for waiver of immunity of Gianluca Buonanno', this report would be put to the vote on Thursday, in accordance with Rule 9(8).

—   Request by the GUE/NGL Group for a statement by the Council on 'The alleged involvement of Commissioner Miguel Arias Cañete in corruption cases' to be added as the first item on the agenda.

   The following spoke: Xabier Benito Ziluaga, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, to move the request, Kathleen Van Brempt and Françoise Grossetête, to oppose the request.

   By RCV (106 in favour, 255 against, 28 abstentions), Parliament rejected the request.

The order of business was thus established.

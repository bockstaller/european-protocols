IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

Sean Kelly had been present at the sittings of 22 November 2010 and 1 February 2012, although his name was not on the attendance register.

The President recalled that José Manuel Barroso (President of the Commission) would present his declaration on the state of the Union (point 36 of the FDA) on Wednesday, 12 September 2012.


3. Tribute

On behalf of Parliament, the President paid tribute to the memory of Jiří Havel MEP, former MEPs Edith Mastenbroek, Alexander Falconer and Patrick Lane, and Oswaldo Payá, winner of the Sakharov Prize in 2002.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.


4. Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the continuing violations of human rights, democracy and the rule of law in Belarus and called on the President, Alexander Lukashenko, immediately to set free, without condition, all political prisoners and ensure that the forthcoming parliamentary elections would be fair and would conform to international standards.

The President announced that he had sent a letter to the President of the Republic of the Gambia after the state execution of nine people in that country. He called on the government to put an end to executions and to reintroduce the moratorium on the death penalty.

The President congratulated Stavros Lambrinidis, former MEP and Vice-President of Parliament, on his appointment to the post of EU Special Representative for Human Rights.


5. Composition of Parliament

The Cyprus authorities had given notice of the election of Sophocles Sophocleous to replace Kyriakos Mavronikolas as Member of Parliament with effect from 1 September 2012.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as his credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that he had previously made a written declaration stating that he did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Sophocles Sophocleous would take his seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

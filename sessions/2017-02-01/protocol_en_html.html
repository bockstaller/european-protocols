11. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the 1 and 2 February 2017 sittings (PE 598.387/OJ) had been distributed and the following changes had been proposed (Rule 149a):

The following spoke: Jo Leinen, Anna Maria Corazza Bildt, Anna Elżbieta Fotyga, Peter Lundgren, Nigel Farage, Notis Marias, Ana Gomes, Janice Atkinson and Nikolay Barekov.

Wednesday

With the agreement of the political groups, the President proposed the following changes:

- to add as the first item on the agenda a statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on travel restrictions following US President executive orders;

- to add as the third item on the agenda a statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on managing migration along the Central Mediterranean route;

- to postpone the debate on biological low-risk pesticides to the February II part-session.

The House agreed to the proposals and the sitting was therefore extended until 24:00.

The ENF Group requested that a statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the secret activities of the EU Intelligence and Situation Centre (INTCEN), the EEAS analysis centre, be added as the second item on the agenda.

The following spoke: Georg Mayer, on behalf of the ENF Group , to move the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

Thursday

The PPE and ALDE Groups requested that a Commission statement on democracy and justice in Romania be added as the first item on the agenda.

The following spoke: Manfred Weber, on behalf of the PPE Group, and Norica Nicolai, on behalf of the ALDE Group, to move the request, Maria João Rodrigues, on behalf of the S&D Group, in support and also to request that the LIBE Committee debate the issue and that a fact-finding mission be sent to Romania.

The House agreed to the requests.

Voting the following day would therefore start only at 11:30 instead of 11.00.

The order of business was thus established.

The following spoke: Rebecca Harms.

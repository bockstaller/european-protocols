3. Election of the President of the European Parliament

The President drew attention to the provisions of Rule 13(1).

He announced that he had received the following nominations in accordance with the Rules of Procedure: Nirj Deva, Martin Schulz and Diana Wallis (minutes of 16.1.2012, item 8).

He reminded the House that, under Rule 14(1), an absolute majority of the votes cast was required for the election of the President. Spoiled ballot papers would not be counted.

The President then drew lots to appoint eight tellers. The following were appointed: Rita Borsellino, Csaba Sógor, Vladko Todorov Panayotov, Pavel Poc, Zoltán Bagó, Jarosław Kalinowski, Ivailo Kalfin and Andres Perello Rodriguez.

The President explained the voting procedure to the House.

The following spoke: Carl Schlyter.

The vote was taken.

(The sitting was suspended at 9.45 for the count and resumed at 10.30.)

IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President


4. Election of the President of the European Parliament (continued)

In the presence of the former Presidents of Parliament Enrique Barón Crespo, Josep Borrell Fontelles, Pat Cox, Nicole Fontaine, Klaus Hänsch and Hans-Gert Pöttering, the President read out the results of the ballot:

- Members voting:      699

- Blank or void:       29

- Votes cast:      670

- Absolute majority:      336

(The list of Members voting is annexed to these Minutes: Annex 1 to the minutes of 17 January 2012).

The results were as follows:

- Nirj Deva      142 votes

- Martin Schulz      387 votes

- Diana Wallis      141 votes

Martin Schulz had obtained an absolute majority of the votes cast. Jerzy Buzek therefore proclaimed him President of the European Parliament and congratulated him on his election.

IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

To mark his election, Martin Schulz made a speech in which he paid homage to his predecessor, Jerzy Buzek, and outlined his programme.

The following spoke: Joseph Daul, on behalf of the PPE Group, Maria Badia i Cutchet, on behalf of the S&D Group, Guy Verhofstadt, on behalf of the ALDE Group, Rebecca Harms, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Martin Callanan, on behalf of the ECR Group, Kartika Tamara Liotard, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Nigel Farage, on behalf of the EFD Group, Barry Madlener, Non-attached Member, and José Manuel Barroso (President of the Commission).

The President made a brief statement.

The item closed.


5. Election of the Vice-Presidents of the European Parliament (deadline for submitting nominations)

At the proposal of the President, the deadline for submitting nominations for election to the posts of Vice-President was set at 18.30 the same day. The election was to take place at 19.30.

(The sitting was suspended at 11.50 and resumed at 19.35.)


IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

6. Election of the Vice-Presidents of the European Parliament

The President announced that he had received the following nominations for election to the office of Vice-President: Alexander Alvaro, Roberta Angelilli, Isabelle Durant, Othmar Karas, Miguel Angel Martínez Martínez, Edward McMillan-Scott, Georgios Papastamkos, Gianni Pittella, Anni Podimata, Jacek Protasiewicz, László Surján, Indrek Tarand, Alejo Vidal-Quadras, Oldřich Vlasák and Rainer Wieland.

As the number of candidates exceeded the number of seats to be filled, the President proposed that the Vice-Presidents be elected by secret ballot, in accordance with Rule 15(1).

The President drew attention to the provisions of Rule 15(1) and explained the voting procedure.

The vote was taken.

(The sitting was suspended at 20.05 for the vote count and resumed at 21.15)

IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ

President

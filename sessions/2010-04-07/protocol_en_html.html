EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT

2010 - 2011 SESSION

Sitting of 7 April 2010

BRUSSELS

MINUTES

WEDNESDAY 7 APRIL 2010

IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 14.35.


2. Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the terrorist attacks of 29 March 2010 on the Moscow underground and expressed his condolences to the victims' families on Parliament's behalf. He added that the European Parliament condemned all terrorist attacks.

The President made a statement regarding the 70th anniversary of the massacre of Polish prisoners by Soviet forces at Katyń.

The President announced that the Commission would be represented by Maroš Šefčovič (Vice-President of the Commission) at that day's debate as José Manuel Barroso (President of the Commission) was unable to attend.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: Francesco Enrico Speroni, on the victims of the recent terrorist attacks in Iraq, Afghanistan and Pakistan. (The President paid tribute to the victims).

°
° ° °

Marian Harkin had made it known that she had been present at the sitting of 24 March 2010 but that her name was not on the attendance register.


4. Conclusions of the European Council meeting (25-26 March 2010) (debate)

European Council and Commission statements: Conclusions of the European Council meeting (25-26 March 2010)

Herman Van Rompuy (President of the European Council) and Maroš Šefčovič (Vice-President of the Commission) made the statements.

The following spoke: Corien Wortmann-Kool, on behalf of the PPE Group, Hannes Swoboda, on behalf of the S&D Group, Guy Verhofstadt, on behalf of the ALDE Group, Rebecca Harms, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Timothy Kirkhope, on behalf of the ECR Group, Lothar Bisky, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Nigel Farage, on behalf of the EFD Group, Barry Madlener, Non-attached Member, Herman Van Rompuy, Maroš Šefčovič, Gunnar Hökmark, Gianluca Susta, Lena Ek, Derk Jan Eppink, Nikolaos Chountis, Mara Bizzotto, Martin Ehrenhauser, Jean-Pierre Audy, Pervenche Berès, Malcolm Harbour, Georgios Toussas, Frank Vanhecke, Marietta Giannakou, Kathleen Van Brempt, Othmar Karas, Juan Fernando López Aguilar, Danuta Maria Hübner, Anni Podimata, José Manuel García-Margallo y Marfil and Csaba Őry.

The following spoke under the 'catch-the-eye' procedure: Seán Kelly, Kriton Arsenis, Norica Nicolai, David Campbell Bannerman, Franz Obermayr and Monica Luisa Macovei.

The following spoke: Maroš Šefčovič and Herman Van Rompuy.

The debate closed.


5. Dates of forthcoming sittings

The next sittings would be held from 19 to 22 April 2010.


6. Adjournment of the session

The session of the European Parliament was adjourned.

The sitting closed at 17.10.

Klaus Welle

Jerzy Buzek

Secretary-General

President

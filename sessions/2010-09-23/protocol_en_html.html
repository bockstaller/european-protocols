IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 10.05.

The following spoke: Bernd Posselt and Richard Seeber on the organisation of the debates.


2. European Neighbourhood and Partnership Instrument and the cross-border cooperation programmes (debate)

Commission statement: European Neighbourhood and Partnership Instrument and the cross-border cooperation programmes

Štefan Füle (Member of the Commission) made the statement.

IN THE CHAIR: Libor ROUČEK
Vice-President

The following spoke: Lambert van Nistelrooij, on behalf of the PPE Group, Evgeni Kirilov, on behalf of the S&D Group, Kristiina Ojuland, on behalf of the ALDE Group, Malika Benarab-Attou, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Konrad Szymański, on behalf of the ECR Group, Marie-Christine Vergiat, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Derek Roland Clark, on behalf of the EFD Group, Andreas Mölzer, Non-attached Member, Elmar Brok, Csaba Sándor Tabajdi, Riikka Manner, Tomasz Piotr Poręba, László Surján, Carl Haglund and Jacek Olgierd Kurski.

The following spoke under the 'catch-the-eye' procedure: Bernd Posselt, Charles Tannock, Jaroslav Paška, Elena Băsescu, Marek Henryk Migalski, Bastiaan Belder, Iosif Matula and Andrew Henry William Brons.

IN THE CHAIR: Rainer WIELAND
Vice-President

The following spoke under the 'catch-the-eye' procedure: Jan Kozłowski, Marian-Jean Marinescu and Michael Gahler.

The following spoke: Štefan Füle.

The debate closed.


3. Import of Laogai-made goods into the EU (debate)

Commission statement: Import of Laogai-made goods into the EU

Štefan Füle (Member of the Commission) made the statement.

The following spoke: Michael Gahler, on behalf of the PPE Group, Karin Kadenbach, on behalf of the S&D Group, Charles Goerens, on behalf of the ALDE Group, Marek Henryk Migalski, on behalf of the ECR Group, Marie-Christine Vergiat, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Lorenzo Fontana, on behalf of the EFD Group, Daniel Caspary, Gesine Meissner, Fiorello Provera, Cristian Dan Preda, Cristiana Muscardini and Tunne Kelam.

The following spoke under the 'catch-the-eye' procedure: Martin Kastler, Michael Theurer, Eva Lichtenberger, Charles Tannock, Jaroslav Paška, Nick Griffin and Andrew Henry William Brons.

The following spoke: Štefan Füle.

The debate closed.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


5. Verification of credentials

On the JURI Committee's proposal, Parliament decided to confirm the mandate of Sergio Gutiérrez Prieto, with effect from 19 July 2010.


6. Composition of committees and delegations

8. Council positions at first reading

The President announced under Rule 61(1) that the following common positions had been received from the Council, together with the reasons which had led to their adoption, and the Commission's position on:

- Position of the Council at first reading with a view to the adoption of a Directive of the European Parliament and of the Council on the application of patients' rights in cross border healthcare (11038/2/2010 - C7-0266/2010 – 2008/0142(COD))
referred back to committee responsible: ENVI

- Position of the Council at first reading with a view to the adoption of a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council laying down harmonised conditions for the marketing of construction products and repealing Council Directive 89/106/EEC (10753/3/2010 - C7-0267/2010 – 2008/0098(COD))
referred back to committee responsible: IMCO

The three-month period available to Parliament to adopt its position would therefore begin the following day (24 September 2010).

11. Forwarding of texts adopted during the sitting

Pursuant to Rule 179(2), the minutes of that day's sitting would be submitted to Parliament for its approval at the beginning of the next sitting.

With Parliament's agreement, the texts that had been adopted would be forwarded forthwith to the bodies named therein.


12. Dates of forthcoming sittings

The next sittings would be held on 6 and 7 October 2010.


13. Adjournment of the session

The session of the European Parliament was adjourned.

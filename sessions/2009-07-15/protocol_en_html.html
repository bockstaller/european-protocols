6.1.  Election of Quaestors of Parliament (first, second and third ballots)

The President announced that he had received the following nominations for the election of Quaestors.

Lidia Joanna Geringer de Oedenberg , Jim Higgins , Astrid Lulling , Jiří Maštálka , Bill Newton Dunn , James Nicholson , Ria Oomen-Ruijten and Francesco Enrico Speroni .

As the number of candidates exceeded the number of seats to be filled, a secret ballot would be held under Rule 13(1) by electronic vote.

Under Rule 16, second paragraph, the same rules would apply to this election would as to the election of vice-presidents.

The House tested the electronic voting system.

The following spoke: Martin Schulz , Sergio Paolo Francesco Silvestris and Carlo Casini , on the voting procedure.

The vote was taken.

First ballot

The President read out the results of the ballot:

- Members voting:      696

- blank or void:      2

- Votes cast:      694

- Absolute majority:      348

Votes obtained were as follows, in descending order:

Lidia Joanna Geringer de Oedenberg    398 votes

Jim Higgins          352 votes

Astrid Lulling       322 votes

Jiří Maštálka       206 votes

James Nicholson       174 votes

Bill Newton Dunn       171 votes

Ria Oomen-Ruijten       161 votes

Francesco Enrico Speroni    145 votes

(The list of Members voting is annexed to these Minutes.)

Lidia Joanna Geringer de Oedenberg and Jim Higgins , had each obtained an absolute majority of the votes cast and were declared Quaestors of Parliament.

As three seats remained to be filled, a second ballot was required. The President noted that the nominations would be the same as for the first ballot.

The vote was held.

Second ballot

The President read out the results of the second ballot:

- Members voting:   699

- blank or void:   2

- Votes cast:   697

- Absolute majority:   349

Votes obtained were as follows, in descending order:

Astrid Lulling       327 votes

Jiří Maštálka       283 votes

Bill Newton Dunn       202 votes

Ria Oomen-Ruijten       187 votes

James Nicholson       172 votes

Francesco Enrico Speroni    130 votes

(The list of Members voting is annexed to these Minutes.)

The President noted that no candidate had obtained an absolute majority. Three seats therefore remained to be filled. The President noted that the nominations would be the same as for the second ballot.

The President recalled that a simple majority (not an absolute majority) was required for the third ballot.

The vote was held.


Third ballot

The President read out the results of the third ballot:

- Members voting:   705

- blank or void:   2

- Votes cast:   703

Votes obtained were as follows, in descending order:

Astrid Lulling       306 votes

Jiří Maštálka       293 votes

Bill Newton Dunn       208 votes

Ria Oomen-Ruijten       186 votes

James Nicholson       176 votes

Francesco Enrico Speroni    131 votes

(The list of Members voting is annexed to these Minutes.)

Astrid Lulling , Jiří Maštálka and Bill Newton Dunn were declared Quaestors of Parliament.

Under Rule 15(2) and Rule 16, the order of precedence was determined by the order in which they were elected .

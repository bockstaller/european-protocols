IN THE CHAIR: Hans-Gert PÖTTERING
President

1.  Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2.  Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the occasion of the fifth anniversary of the EU's enlargement to include ten new countries in 2004.

°
° ° °

The President made a statement expressing his anxiety at the fate of the American-Iranian journalist Roxana Saberi, currently imprisoned in Iran. On behalf of Parliament, he added his voice to those of the President-in-Office of the Council and of the President of the USA in calling on the Iranian authorities for the immediate and unconditional release of Roxana Saberi.

He also protested against the execution in Iran of a woman for a crime committed when she was still a minor.

°
° ° °

The President expressed Parliament's solidarity with the Queen of the Netherlands and with the Dutch people following the incident in Apeldoorn on 30 April 2009 in which several people died..


3.  Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

The following spoke: Johannes Blokland on the last of the President's statements.


4.  Verification of credentials

The President announced that, in its judgment of 30 April 2009 on joined cases C-393/07 (Italian Republic v European Parliament) and C-9/08 (Beniamino Donnici v European Parliament), the European Court of Justice:

'Annuls Decision 2007/2121(REG) of the European Parliament of 24 May 2007 on the verification of credentials of Mr Beniamino Donnici '.

Concerning the credentials of Beniamino Donnici , the legal situation that had existed immediately before the adoption of the annulled decision was therefore reestablished. As a result of the judgment of the Court, the European Parliament was required to verify the credentials of Beniamino Donnici again.

The matter was therefore again referred to the JURI Committee as the committee responsible.

7.  Announcement by the President

The President announced that, under the provisions of the Rules of Procedure relating to unfinished business at the end of the parliamentary term (Rule 203), Parliament had always followed the consistent practice of deeming all questions for written answer that had not received an answer on the last day of the last session of the term to have lapsed.

However, as President Cox had pointed out at the close of the fifth term, this practice had no legal basis under the current wording of Rule 203.

The President therefore called on the Council and the Commission to answer all questions for written answer tabled by Members up to the end of the present session (7 May 2009).

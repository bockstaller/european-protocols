EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT

2009 - 2010 SESSION

Sittings of 24 and 25 February 2010

BRUSSELS

MINUTES

WEDNESDAY 24 FEBRUARY 2010

IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 15.05.


2. Statements by the President

The President paid tribute to the memory of Parliament official Claudia Candeago, who had died in the railway accident in Belgium on 15 February 2010.

The President made a statement on the floods and landslides that had struck Madeira on 20 February 2010.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.


3. Welcome

On behalf of Parliament, the President welcomed Andżelika Borys, President of the Union of Poles in Belarus, and Alyaksandr Milinkevich, winner of the 2006 Sakharov Prize, who had taken their seats in the distinguished visitors' gallery.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

Martin Ehrenhauser had made it known that he had been present at the sitting of 18 January 2010 but that his name was not on the attendance register.

Frédérique Ries had made it known that she had taken part in the votes of 20 January 2010 but that, for technical reasons, her votes had not been fully recorded.


5. Transportation of horses for slaughter in the European Union (written declaration)

Written Declaration 0054/2009 by Elizabeth Lynne, Sidonia Elżbieta Jędrzejewska and Carl Schlyter, on the transportation of horses for slaughter in the European Union, had been signed by a majority of Parliament's component Members. In accordance with Rule 123(3) and (4), it would therefore be forwarded to its addressees and published in the Texts Adopted of the sitting of 25 February 2010, and the names of the signatories would be listed in the minutes.

The following spoke: Elizabeth Lynne.


6. Composition of Parliament

The French authorities had given notice of the appointment of Constance Le Grip to replace Michel Barnier as Member of Parliament with effect from 10 February 2010.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as her credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that she had previously made a written declaration stating that she did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Constance Le Grip would take her seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

12. Order of business

The next item was the order of business.

The final draft agenda for the February II sittings (PE 439.049/PDOJ) had been distributed and a number of changes had been proposed (Rule 140):

Wednesday

1. The sitting would be held without suspension from 15.00 to 24.00.

2. The items entitled 'Parliament's priorities for the UN Human Rights Council (Geneva, 1-26 March 2010)' (item 10 on the final draft agenda) and 'Implementation of Goldstone recommendations on Israel/Palestine' (item 23 on the final draft agenda) would not be based on statements by the High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and Vice-President of the Commission but on Commission and Council statements.

3. Council and Commission statements on the situation of civil society and of national minorities in Belarus would be added immediately after the debate on implementation of the Goldstone recommendations on Israel/Palestine. The vote would be held in March.

4. Immediately after the one-minute speeches, a Commission statement would be added on the major natural disaster in the Autonomous Region of Madeira. The vote would be held in March.

Thursday

1. During the debate on the report by Maria do Céu Patrão Neves on the Green Paper on reform of the common fisheries policy (A7-0014/2010) (item 4 on the final draft agenda), the Council would be allocated five minutes' speaking time.

2. After the debate on the report by Maria do Céu Patrão Neves , a Commission statement would be added on rail safety, including the European signalling system. There would be no tabling of motions for resolutions.

3. Voting would begin at 11.30.

4. Request by the S&D Group to hold over the vote on the report by Klaus-Heiner Lehne (A7-0011/2010) on annual accounts of certain types of companies as regards micro-entities.

The following spoke: Martin Schulz, on behalf of the S&D Group, who moved the request, Klaus-Heiner Lehne, Dirk Sterckx and Martin Schulz.

Parliament agreed to the request.

IN THE CHAIR: Antonio TAJANI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday 8 February 2018 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 15.04.


3. Statements by the President

The President made a statement forcefully condemning the murders of Slovakian investigative girlfriend Ján Kuciak and his partner.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.

The President, on Parliament's behalf, condemned the kidnapping by Boko Haram of more than 100 girls in north-eastern Nigeria.

The President told the House that he had made representations to the Bulgarian Government in defence of the right of Verts/ALE Group Co-President Ska Keller to freedom of expression following an incident involving Bulgarian Deputy Prime Minister Valeri Simeonov.

The following spoke: Bruno Gollnisch, Cécile Kashetu Kyenge, Lefteris Christoforou and Liliana Rodrigues.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


5. Election of a Vice-President of the European Parliament (nominations)

The President announced, in accordance with Rule 15, that the following nominations had been received for the vacant post of Vice-President of Parliament:

- Zdzisław Krasnodębski, nominated by the ECR Group,

- Indrek Tarand, nominated by a number of Members reaching at least the low threshold.

The election would take place at 11.30 the following day, Thursday 1 March 2018 (minutes of 1.3.2018, item 8.1).


6. Composition of Parliament

The competent Finnish authorities had informed Parliament that Hannu Takkula had been appointed as a Member of the European Court of Auditors with effect from 1 March 2018.

The following spoke: Hannu Takkula.

°
° ° °

The competent Spanish, Finnish and Latvian authorities had given notice that Ana Miranda, Elsi Katainen and Miroslavs Mitrofanovs had been elected to the European Parliament with effect from, respectively, 28 February, 1 March and 5 March 2018 to replace, respectively, Iosu Juaristi Abaunz, Hannu Takkula and Tatjana Ždanoka.

Parliament took note of their election.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Ana Miranda, Elsi Katainen and Miroslavs Mitrofanovs would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

°
° ° °

Janusz Korwin-Mikke had given notice in writing of his resignation as Member of Parliament, with effect from 2 March 2018.

In accordance with Rule 4(1) and (2), Parliament had noted that the seat was vacant from that date and had informed the national authority concerned.


7. Verification of credentials

On a proposal from the JURI Committee, Parliament validated the mandates of Geoffroy Didier and Gabriela Zoană, with effect from 1 December 2017 and 30 January 2018, respectively.


8. Composition of political groups

David Borrelli was no longer a member of the EFDD Group as of 13 February 2018. He was now a non-attached Member.

Rikke Karlsson was no longer a member of the ECR Group as of 21 February 2018. She was now a non-attached Member.

Julie Girling and Richard Ashworth were no longer members of the ECR Group as of 28 February 2018, and had joined the PPE Group.


9. Composition of committees and delegations

At the request of the PPE, GUE/NGL and ENF Groups, Parliament ratified the following appointments:

JURI Committee: Ana Miranda to replace Jiří Maštálka

Delegation for relations with the United States: Ana Miranda

AGRI Committee: Jacques Colombier

Delegation to the EU-Turkey Joint Parliamentary Committee: Jacques Colombier.

16. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the February II plenary sittings (PE 617.959/PDOJ) had been distributed.

Having consulted the political groups, the President proposed the following changes (Rule 149):

Wednesday

A statement by the President on the humanitarian situation in Syria, in particular Ghouta, followed by one round of political group speakers, would be added as the first item on the agenda.

This would be followed by the European Council informal meeting of 23 February 2018.

A statement by the Vice-President of the Commission and High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the EU priorities for the UN Human Rights Council sessions in 2018 would be added as the fourth item on the agenda.

The statement by the Vice-President of the Commission and High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the EU's perspectives for the first EU-Cuba Joint Council meeting (item 29 on the final draft agenda) would be postponed.

Thursday

The order of the first two items on the agenda would be reversed: the Loones report (item 4 on the final draft agenda) would thus become the first item on the agenda and the Maurel report the second (item 14 on the final draft agenda).

An address by Nils Muižnieks, Council of Europe Commissioner for Human Rights, would be added to the agenda at 11.30, before voting time.

The following would be added to the list of votes:

- Report by Roberto Gualtieri on the appointment of a member of the Single Resolution Board (A8-0030/2018)

- Objection pursuant to Rule 106: authorisation of genetically modified maize 59122 (DAS-59122-7) (B8-0122/2018)

- Objection pursuant to Rule 106: authorisation of genetically modified maize MON 87427 × MON 89034 × NK603 and genetically modified maize combining two of the events MON 87427, MON 89034 and NK603 (B8-0124/2018).

Parliament approved these changes.

Wednesday

Request from the GUE/NGL Group to add, as the eighth item on the agenda, after the statement on the work and legacy of the International Criminal Tribunal for the Former Yugoslavia (item 30 on the final draft agenda), a Commission statement on former Commission President Barroso's prohibited lobby activities, such as the meeting with Commissioner Katainen.

The following spoke: Dennis de Jong, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, to move the request.

Parliament approved the request by roll-call vote (241 in favour, 133 against, 8 abstentions).

The following spoke: Victor Boştinaru to state that he had intended to vote in favour.

Thursday

Request from the ENF Group to include on the agenda a joint debate about jihadists, including, as well as the report by Javier Nart (item 16 on the final draft agenda), statements by the Council and the Commission on 'jihadist brides returning to Europe'.

The following spoke: Janice Atkinson, on behalf of the ENF Group, to move the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

The order of business was thus established.

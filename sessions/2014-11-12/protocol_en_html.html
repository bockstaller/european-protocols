4. Composition of Parliament

Miguel Arias Cañete, Andrus Ansip, Valdis Dombrovskis, Christos Stylianides, Marianne Thyssen and Corina Crețu had been appointed Members of the Commission with effect from 1 November 2014.

Under Rule 4(1) and (4) Parliament established that their seats were vacant from that date and informed the national authorities concerned.

° ° °

Under Rule 4(1) and (4) Parliament established that his seat was vacant from that date and informed the national authorities concerned.

°
° ° °

Francisco Sosa Wagner had given notice in writing of his resignation as Member of Parliament, with effect from 20 October 2014.

Under Rule 4(3) Parliament established that his seat was vacant from that date and informed the national authorities concerned.

° ° °

The Estonian authorities had given notice of the election of Urmas Paet to replace Andrus Ansip as Member of Parliament with effect from 3 November 2014.

The Cypriot authorities had given notice of the election of Lefteris Christoforou to replace Christos Stylianides as Member of Parliament with effect from 3 November 2014.

The Latvian authorities had given notice of the election of Inese Vaidere to replace Valdis Dombrovskis as Member of Parliament with effect from 1 November 2014.

The Spanish authorities had given notice of the election of Carlos Iturgaiz to replace Miguel Arias Cañete as Member of Parliament with effect from 6 November 2014.

The Belgian authorities had given notice of the election of Tom Vandenkendelaere to replace Marianne Thyssen as Member of Parliament with effect from 6 November 2014.

Under Article 7(1) of the Act of 20 September 1976 concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage and Rule 4(1) and (4), Parliament took note of the election of Urmas Paet and Lefteris Christoforou with effect from 3 November 2014, Inese Vaidere with effect from 1 November 2014, and Carlos Iturgaiz and Tom Vandenkendelaere with effect from 6 November 2014.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Urmas Paet, Lefteris Christoforou, Carlos Iturgaiz, Tom Vandenkendelaere and Inese Vaidere would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

15. Election of a Vice-President of the European Parliament (vote)

The President said that Ioan Mircea Pașcu and Fabio Massimo Castaldo were candidates.

Fabio Massimo Castaldo withdrew as a candidate.

The President decided to hold the vote by secret electronic ballot.

The following spoke: on the voting procedure: Ashley Fox, Anne-Marie Mineur, Nuno Melo and Richard Sulík.

The vote was held.

Parliament elected Ioan Mircea Pașcu by EV (461 Members voting, 284 votes in favour, 177 abstentions).

(The list of Members voting is annexed to these Minutes (minutes of 12.11.2014 annex 1).)

The President pronounced Ioan Mircea Pașcu Vice-President of the European Parliament and congratulated him on his election.

Under Rule 20(1), he took the place of the outgoing Vice-President in order of precedence and thus became the eighth Vice-President.   

The following spoke: Jonathan Arnott.

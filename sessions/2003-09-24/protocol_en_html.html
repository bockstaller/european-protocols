1. Opening of sitting

The sitting opened at 09.00.

The following spoke:

- Ilda Figueiredo, who asked the President to make a final effort to save Amina Lawal, whose appeal would be heard the following day by the Upper Sharia Court (the President replied that the President of Parliament had already contacted the Nigerian authorities);

- Patricia McKenna, who stated that France had refused to give visas to a group of disabled people from Belgrade who were due to visit Parliament in connection with the European Year for the Disabled (the President replied that the matter would be raised with the French authorities).

5. Patentability of computer-implemented inventions ***I (vote)

Report on the proposal for a directive of the European Parliament and of the Council on the patentability of computer-implemented inventions [COM(2002) 92 - C5-0082/2002 - 2002/0047(COD)] - Committee on Legal Affairs and the Internal Market. Rapporteur: Arlene McCarthy (A5-0238/2003)

(Simple majority)

(Voting record: Annex 1, Item 2)

COMMISSION PROPOSAL

Approved as amended (P5_TA(2003)0402)

DRAFT LEGISLATIVE RESOLUTION

Adopted (P5_TA(2003)0402)

The following spoke:

- Johanna L.A. Boogerd-Quaak, to clarify amendment 98.

- Raina A. Mercedes Echerer, who pointed out that amendments 57, 99 and 110 (which were identical) went further than amendment 100, and suggested that they be voted first (the President consulted the rapporteur, who did not agree, and thus the suggestion was not acted upon).

- the rapporteur, who, having been consulted by the President, indicated that the adoption of the first part of amendment 100 would not cause amendments 57, 99 and 110 (which were identical) to fall;

- Piia-Noora Kauppi, who moved an oral amendment to amendment 110;

- the rapporteur, who, having been consulted by the President, indicated that the adoption of amendments 102 and 111 (which were identical) would not cause amendments 72 and 18 to fall;

- the rapporteur, who, having been consulted by the President, indicated that the adoption of amendment 76 would not cause amendment 20 to fall;

- Brian Crowley who considered that amendment 50 should therefore also be put to the vote; the rapporteur, changing her mind, then agreed that amendments 50 and 20 fell.

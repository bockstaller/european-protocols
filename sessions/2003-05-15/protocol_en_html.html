IN THE CHAIR: Renzo IMBENI
Vice-President

Gérard Onesta raised what he believed to be an infringement of Articles 1, 2 and 3 of Chapter 2 of the rules in force since 1 July 1998 governing the holding of meetings concurrently with part-sessions. The European Convention was holding a meeting that day, thereby obliging, in principle, 32 Members to leave for Brussels, which risked, he said, distorting the result of the midday votes. He opined that, exceptionally, the Convention could have met in Strasbourg and called for the necessary measures to be taken to ensure that such a situation would not recur during the part-session of 5 June 2003 (the President suggested he address his observations to the President of the Convention).


4. Deadline for tabling amendments to SAB 1 and 2/2003

The President announced that the deadline for tabling amendments to SAB 1 and 2 for the 2003 financial year was 17.00 on 19 May 2003.

5. Agenda

The President proposed, on the basis of Rule 111(2), to deal with the Ingo Schmitt report on customs cooperation to combat money laundering (A5-0073/2003) without debate and to put it to the vote at midday.

Bernd Posselt considered that the report should have been debated that afternoon, as planned, and voted on during voting time at 17.30; Ingo Schmitt, (rapporteur), supported the President's proposal and called for the vote on his report, which was uncontroversial, to take place at midday.

Parliament approved the proposal.

IN THE CHAIR: Alejo VIDAL-QUADRAS ROCA
Vice-President

The following spoke on the decision taken previously by Parliament to vote without debate the Schmitt report on customs cooperation to combat money laundering (A5-0073/2003) (see item 5):

- Bernd Posselt, who requested the President clarify what Rule the decision was based on (the President replied that it was based on Rule 111(2), subparagraph 1, which he then read out);

- Rainer Wieland, who expressed his support for Bernd Posselt's remarks;

- Bernd Posselt, who, referring to the President's reply to his previous remarks, contested the validity of taking such a decision on the basis of the rule in question and asked whether, in that instance, what was involved was an off-the-cuff proposal by the President or a proposal based on a decision of the Conference of Presidents (the President replied that the rapporteur, Ingo Schmitt, had consulted the Chair in advance and that it had been decided that, if a majority of the Members were in favour, the matter would be dealt with in that way).

Efstratios Korakas condemned the attack carried out on 10 May 2003 by the Israeli army in Ramallah, Palestine, on the offices of the Palestinian People's Party; he expressed his solidarity with that party and with the Palestinian people as a whole and asked for Parliament's support.

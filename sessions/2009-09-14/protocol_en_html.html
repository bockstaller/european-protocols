IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Statements by the President

The President made a statement about the ceremonies commemorating the 70th anniversary of the start of the Second World War.

°
° ° °

He paid tribute to the memory of the two members of the Spanish Guardia Civil murdered in the summer by ETA.

He paid tribute to the memory of Ernest Glinne and Christopher Prout, former Members of the European Parliament, who had passed away on 10 August 2009 and 12 July 2009 respectively.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in honour of the deceased.


3. Composition of Parliament

The Belgian authorities had informed Parliament of the appointment of Jean-Claude Marcourt to an office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, in accordance with Article 7(3) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage.

Under Rule 4(1) and (4), Parliament established that his seat had been vacant with effect from 16 July 2009.

°
° ° °

The Bulgarian authorities had informed Parliament of the appointment of Rumiana Jeleva as Minister of Foreign Affairs of Bulgaria with effect from 27 July 2009.

Since, pursuant to Article 7(1) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, this office was incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Parliament established the vacancy under Rule 4(4) with effect from 27 July 2009 and informed the Member State concerned thereof.

°
° ° °

The Belgian authorities had given notice of the election to the European Parliament of Marc Tarabella to replace Jean-Claude Marcourt. Under Rule 4(1) and (4) Parliament established the vacancy with effect from 16 July 2009.

The Bulgarian authorities had given notice of the election to the European Parliament of Andrey Kovatchev to replace Rumiana Jeleva. Under Rule 4(1) Parliament established the vacancy with effect from 24 August 2009.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Marc Tarabella and Andrey Kovatchev would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

IN THE CHAIR: Antonio TAJANI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday 31 January 2019 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.08.


3. Statements by the President

The President announced that Parliament's radio studio 1 in Strasbourg had been re-named in honour of the journalists Antonio Megalizzi and Bartosz Piotr Orent-Niedzielski, who had been killed in the terrorist attack in Strasbourg in December 2018. The President also welcomed the families of the journalists to the official gallery and underlined Parliament's commitment to press freedom.

The President then spoke about the situation of journalists held in detention in Venezuela and called on behalf of Parliament for their release.

The President informed Parliament about the hounding of former MEP Feleknas Uca in Turkey and said he would contact the Turkish authorities.

The President made a statement to mark his participation in the 'Giorno del ricordo' in Italy, in memory of the victims of the 'foibe' massacres.

The following spoke Ivan Jakovčić, Franc Bogovič (the President gave explanations) and Ruža Tomašić (the President gave explanations).

The following spoke Ramón Jáuregui Atondo on the situation in Nicaragua (the President replied that he would contact the relevant authorities), Steven Woolfe, Gabriel Mato, to agree with what was said by Ramón Jáuregui Atondo, Jean-Marie Cavada on what was said by Steven Woolfe, Peter van Dalen on the case of Asia Bibi in Pakistan (the President gave explanations) and Salvatore Cicu.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 30 and 31 January 2019 were approved.

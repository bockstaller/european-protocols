IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday 19 December 2019 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.02.


3. Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the incident involving the shooting-down of a Ukrainian aircraft in Teheran the previous Wednesday and extended Parliament's condolences to the families of the 176 victims in this tragic incident.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of the victims.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: Rasa Juknevičienė.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


5. Composition of committees and delegations

The President had received from the PPE and ECR Groups the following decisions changing the composition of the committees and delegations:

ITRE Committee: Beata Szydło to replace Geoffrey Van Orden

Delegation for relations with the United States: Ioan-Rareş Bogdan to replace Gheorghe-Vlad Nistor

The decisions took effect as of that day.


6. Composition of Parliament

Karoline Edtstadler has notified the President of her appointment as a member of the Government of Austria with effect from 7 January 2020.

Parliament noted that, under Article 7(1) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, this position was incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament and, under Rule 4(1) and (4), declared her seat vacant with effect from 7 January 2020.

°
° ° °

Following the judgment of the Court of Justice of the European Union of 19 December 2019, Parliament took note of the election of Oriol Junqueras i Vies, Carles Puigdemont i Casamajó and Antoni Comín i Oliveres as Members of the European Parliament representing Spain with effect from 2 July 2019.

Given the decision of the Junta Electoral Central of 3 January 2020 and the decision of the Tribunal Supremo of 9 January 2020, Parliament declared Mr Junqueras i Vies's seat vacant with effect from 3 January 2020, in accordance with the Rules of Procedure.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been delivered on any disputes, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Antoni Comín i Oliveres and Carles Puigdemont i Casamajó would take their seats at Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the attendant rights.


7. Composition of political groups

Lucy Elizabeth Harris, Annunziata Mary Rees-Mogg and John Longworth had ceased to be non-attached Members and had joined the ECR Group as of 10 January 2020.

Carles Puigdemont i Casamajó and Antoni Comín i Oliveres were non-attached Members.

12. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the January I 2020 sittings (PE 646.106/PDOJ) had been distributed.

After consulting the political groups, the President proposed the following change (Rule 158):

Thursday

The title of the debate on 'Burundi, notably the case of imprisoned journalists' (item 60 on the final draft agenda) would be changed to 'Burundi, notably freedom of expression'.

The House agreed to the proposal.

The President had also received the following requests for change:

Monday

Request from the PPE Group to change the title of the Commission statement on 'Cross-border organised crime and its impact on free movement' (item 42 on the final draft agenda) to 'Cross-border organised crime and its impact on free movement, the case of case of Malmö and Copenhagen'.
The following spoke: Tomas Tobé, on behalf of the PPE Group, who moved the request, and Iratxe García Pérez against the request.

Parliament rejected the request by roll-call vote (187 for, 258 against, 20 abstentions).

Request from the ID Group to change the title of the Commission statement on 'Devastating Bushfires in Australia and other extreme weather events as a consequence of Climate Change' (item 66 on the final draft agenda) to 'Devastating bush fires in Australia: activation of the RescEU programme'.
The following spoke: Joëlle Mélin, on behalf of the ID Group, who moved the request, and Petros Kokkalis against the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

Wednesday

Request from the GUE/NGL Group to include, as a second item on the afternoon's agenda, Council and Commission statements on 'Excessive use of force by law enforcement authorities during demonstrations and peaceful protests'. The debate would be wound up with the tabling of motions for resolutions, which would be put to the vote on Thursday 16 January 2020.
The following spoke: Anne-Sophie Pelletier, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, who moved the request, Beata Kempa, also on the previous speaker's remarks, and Iratxe García Pérez against the motion to wind up the debate with the tabling of motions for resolutions.

Parliament rejected the request by roll-call vote (220 for, 237 against, 24 abstentions).

Request from the ECR Group to include, as a second item on the afternoon's agenda, a Commission statement on 'The rule of law in France, in particular the recent violence against demonstrating lawyers'. The debate would be wound up with the tabling of motions for resolutions, which would be put to the vote on Thursday 16 January 2020.
The following spoke: Beata Kempa, on behalf of the ECR Group, who moved the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

The order of business was thus established.

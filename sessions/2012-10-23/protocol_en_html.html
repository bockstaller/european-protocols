6.2. Draft general budget of the European Union for the year 2013 (vote)

Draft amendments concerning appropriations under Section III of the general budget of the European Union for the financial year 2013 (Commission)

Draft amendments concerning appropriations under Sections I, II, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII and IX of the general budget of the European Union for the financial year 2013 (European Parliament, Council, Court of Justice, Court of Auditors, Economic and Social Committee, Committee of the Regions, European Ombudsman and European Data Protection Supervisor).

Under the Treaties, draft amendments require for adoption the votes of a majority of Parliament's component Members.

(The draft amendments adopted are listed in an annex to the Texts Adopted)

(Voting record: 'Results of votes', Item 2)

The following had spoken:

Before the vote

Giovanni La Via (general rapporteur on the budget), who said that, as was the case every year, the complexity of the procedure had given rise to the need for certain technical adjustments and that he had therefore proposed the following technical modifications, which Parliament had approved:

Firstly, the only reference document concerning the amounts was DOC 6. In the case of multiple amendments, the vote related to all the lines concerned by the amendment.

Secondly, rectifications should be made in respect of the following pilot projects and preparatory actions:

- Pilot project Development of indicators to measure the implementation of the European Charter for Equality of Women and Men in Local Life (line 33 06 09) adoption under heading 3a and not heading 1a;

- Preparatory action Fruit and vegetable consumption (line 17 03 19) adoption under heading 2 and not heading 3b;

- Pilot project Promotion of Self Care Systems in the European Union (line 17 03 17) correct payment amount EUR 1 million rather than EUR 100 000;

- Preparatory action Climate mainstreaming and innovation (line 07 13 03) reduction of EUR 2 million in commitments and payments compared to the draft budget;

- Preparatory action European voluntary humanitarian aid corps (line 23 02 04) reduction of EUR 1 million in commitments and payments compared to the draft budget.'

After inviting the House to vote, the rapporteur said that Parliament's position did not take into account amending letter No 1/2013, adopted on 19 October 2012 by the Commission, which would form part of the conciliation process.

The rapporteur said it was more important than ever that Parliament show unity and cohesion with regard to the 2013 budget as a difficult conciliation process lay ahead with the Council, which did not seem prepared to free up the necessary resources.

He called on Parliament to send a strong message in its vote, showing that it was united and would fight its corner.

After the vote

Andreas Mavroyiannis (President-in-Office of the Council) took note of the differences between Parliament's position and that of the Council and agreed to the conciliation committee being convened under Article 314(4)(c) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.

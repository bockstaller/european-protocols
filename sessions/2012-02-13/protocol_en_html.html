1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

Marian Harkin had been present at the sitting of 1 February 2012, but her name was not on the attendance register.

The minutes of the sittings of 1 February 2012 and 2 February 2012 were approved.


3. Composition of Parliament

The United Kingdom authorities had given notice of the election of Phil Bennion to replace Elizabeth Lynne as Member of the European Parliament.

Parliament took note of this, with effect from 6 February 2012.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as his credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that he had previously made a written declaration stating that he did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Phil Bennion would take his seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

Kurt Lechner had submitted his resignation as Member of the European Parliament with effect from 17 March 2012.

In accordance with Rule 4(1), Parliament had established the vacancy of his seat with effect from that date and informed the relevant national authority.

13. Statements by the President

The President referred to the case of the Saudi journalist Hamza Kashgari, who was facing execution for remarks posted on Twitter. The President stressed the need to protect freedom of expression and undertook to do all he could to prevent a death sentence being pronounced.

He also expressed his concern at the items posted on an internet site created by the Dutch political party PVV to collect complaints from the public about Central and Eastern European workers living in the Netherlands. He said that he would raise this issue with the Prime Minister of the Netherlands shortly.

The following spoke on the latter issue: Corien Wortmann-Kool, Francesco Enrico Speroni and Sophia in 't Veld.

IN THE CHAIR: Martin SCHULZ
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 15.00.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


3. Requests for the waiver of parliamentary immunity

The Greek authorities had forwarded a request to waive the parliamentary immunity of Theodoros Zagorakis in relation to criminal proceedings in a court in Thessaloniki.

The Polish authorities had forwarded a request to waive the parliamentary immunity of Janusz Korwin-Mikke in relation to criminal proceedings initiated by the Warsaw District Prosecutor.

Pursuant to Rule 9(1), these requests had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.


4. Composition of political groups

The President announced that Amjad Bashir was no longer a member of the EFDD Group and had joined the ECR Group as from 23 January 2015.


5. Texts of agreements forwarded by the Council

11. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the January II 2015 part-session (PE 547.538/PDOJ) had been distributed and the following changes had been proposed (Rule 152):

- Request by the PPE Group that the debate on the Commission statement on "Anti-terrorism measures" (2015/2530(RSP)) (item 14 of the final draft agenda) be closed with the tabling of a motion for a resolution to be voted on during the February I part-session.

The following spoke: Esteban González Pons, on behalf of the PPE Group, who explained the reasons for the request, Guy Verhofstadt, who supported the request, and Birgit Sippel, who opposed it.

Parliament agreed to the request by electronic vote (205 in favour, 140 against, 15 abstentions),

- Request by the Verts/ALE Group to include a Commission statement on the escalation of the conflict in Ukraine and the attacks on Mariupol on the agenda, without the tabling of motions for resolutions, placing this before the item 'One-minute speeches on matters of political importance' and thereby extending the sitting until 21.00.

The following spoke: Rebecca Harms, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, who explained the reasons for the request, and Cristian Dan Preda, who opposed it.

Parliament rejected the request.

The order of business was thus established.

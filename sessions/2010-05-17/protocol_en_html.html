EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT

2010 - 2011 SESSION

Sittings of 17 to 20 May 2010

STRASBOURG

MINUTES

LUNDI 17 MAI 2010

IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the occasion of the Sixth International Day Against Homophobia.

The President referred to Europe Day and Parliament's Open Days, which had seen over 33 000 visitors in Strasbourg and Brussels on 8 and 9 May 2010.

The President informed the House that he had been present in Aachen on 13 May when Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk had been awarded the Charlemagne Prize. He had also attended the ceremony for the award of the European Charlemagne Youth Prize to a German project on 11 May 2010.

The President pointed out that he had taken part in the Conference of Speakers of the European Union Parliaments in Stockholm from 13 to 15 May 2010.

The President mentioned the recent general elections in Hungary and the United Kingdom.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

The following spoke: Mario Mauro and Gianni Pittella, who pointed out that two Italian soldiers had been killed that day in an attack near Herat in Afghanistan; Geoffrey Van Orden, who echoed the sentiments expressed by the two previous speakers and went on to highlight the role of British soldiers involved in NATO's mission in Afghanistan; and Jacky Hénin, who mentioned the victims of accidents at work.

The President paid tribute to all the European soldiers fighting terrorism and violence in Afghanistan and throughout the world.

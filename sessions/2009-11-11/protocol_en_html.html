IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK

1. Formal sitting - 20th anniversary of the democratic change in Central and Eastern Europe

In the presence of Václav Havel, former President of the Czech Republic, a formal sitting of Parliament was held from 15.05 to 15.50 on the occasion of the 20th anniversary of the democratic change in Central and Eastern Europe.


IN THE CHAIR: Gianni PITTELLA
Vice-President

2. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 15.50.


3. Tribute

The President paid tribute, on behalf of Parliament, to the memory of the former Member Lady Diana Elles, who had passed away on 17 October 2009.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

Werner Langen had indicated that he had taken part in all the roll-call votes on 16 September 2009 but that, for technical reasons, only one vote had been recorded.


5. Requests for the defence of parliamentary immunity

Viktor Uspaskich had addressed to the President a request to defend his privileges and immunities in connection with a judicial procedure under way at Vilnius Regional Court.

Clemente Mastella had addressed to the President a request to defend his privileges and immunities in connection with a procedure under way with the Italian judicial authorities.

Under Rule 6(3), these requests had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.

12. Order of business

The next item was the order of business.

The final draft agenda for the November I sittings (PE 429.514/PDOJ) had been distributed and a number of changes were proposed by the President, in agreement with the political groups (Rule 140):

Sittings of 11.11.2009 and 12.11.2009

1. Withdrawing from the agenda Oral Question O-0108/2009 to the Commission (item 11 on the final draft agenda).

2. Placing on the agenda (with debate), after the one-minute speeches, the report by Tanja Fajon on the proposal for a Council regulation amending Regulation (EC) No 539/2001 listing the third countries whose nationals must be in possession of visas when crossing the external borders and those whose nationals are exempt from that requirement (A7-0042/2009), adopted by the LIBE Committee.

3. Amendment of the title of the Commission statement on the political situation in Honduras with a view to the elections on 29 November 2009 (item 14 on the final draft agenda) to 'Commission statement – Situation in Honduras'.

The following spoke: Ioannis Kasoulides, Ulrike Lunacek and Alojz Peterle on the amending the title of the Commission statement on Honduras.

The following spoke: Agustín Díaz de Mera García Consuegra on the voting procedure.

By electronic vote (109 in favour, 142 against, 1 abstention), Parliament rejected the proposal.

4. Placing on the agenda, during Thursday's voting time, the motion for a resolution by the EMPL Committee on the draft Commission decision regarding the PROGRESS annual plan of work 20 (B7-0135/2009), adopted under Rule 88.

The President established that there were no objections to proposed changes 1, 2 and 4 above.

The order of business was thus established.

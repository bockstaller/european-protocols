IN THE CHAIR: Hans-Gert PÖTTERING

1.  Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2.  Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


3.  Follow-up to a request for the defence of immunity

At its meetings of 26-27 March and 8 April 2008, the JURI Committee had considered the request for the defence of the immunity of Tatjana Ždanoka after she had been the victim of an incident while visiting Estonia, which was referred to Parliament on 18 February 2008 ( minutes of 18.02.2008, item 5 ).

The JURI Committee had concluded that Tatjana Ždanoka 's request could not be considered a question of immunity, but regretted that the Protocol on privileges and immunities did not allow the European Parliament to take appropriate measures to respond to cases of this type.

6.  Corrigendum (Rule 204a)

The committee responsible had forwarded the following corrigendum to a European Parliament and Council regulation:

- Corrigendum to Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 18 December 2006 concerning the Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals (REACH), establishing a European Chemicals Agency, amending Directive 1999/45/EC and repealing Council Regulation (EEC) No 793/93 and Commission Regulation (EC) No 1488/94 as well as Council Directive 76/769/EEC and Commission Directives 91/155/EEC, 93/67/EEC, 93/105/EC and 2000/21/EC

Pursuant to Rule 204a(4) of the Rules of Procedure, this corrigendum would be deemed approved unless, not later than 48 hours after its announcement, a request was made by a political group or at least forty Members that it be put to the vote.

The corrigendum was available on the Séance en direct site.

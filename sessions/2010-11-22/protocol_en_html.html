1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

Alfredo Pallone had been present at the sitting of 10 November 2010, but his name did not appear on the attendance register.


3. Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the occasion of the liberation of the Burmese opposition leader Aung San Suu Kyi. He pointed out that she had been awarded the Sakharov Prize in 1990 but had never been able to come in person to collect her prize. He added that he had invited Aung San Suu Kyi to attend a forthcoming part-session of the European Parliament.

The President made a statement to mark the 11th UN International Day for the Elimination of Violence against Women on 25 November 2010.


4. Composition of Parliament

The Spanish authorities had given notice of the election to the European Parliament of María Irigoyen Pérez to replace Ramón Jáuregui Atondo. Parliament took note with effect from 16 November 2010.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as her credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that she had previously made a written declaration stating that she did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, María Irigoyen Pérez would take her seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.


5. Composition of political groups

Parliament noted that Claudiu Ciprian Tănăsescu and Pino Arlacchi had joined the S&D Group with effect from 15 November and 18 November 2010 respectively.

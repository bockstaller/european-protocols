STRASBOURG

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 1 February 2017 and 2 February 2017 were approved.

° ° °

3. Composition of Parliament

Martin Schulz had given notice in writing of his resignation as Member of Parliament, with effect from 11 February 2017.

In accordance with Rule 4(1), (2) and (3), Parliament established the vacancy of his seat with effect from that date and would inform the competent national authority.

10. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the February and II 2017 sittings (PE 598.638/PDOJ) had been distributed and the following changes had been proposed (Rule 149a):

Monday

No changes.

Tuesday

Request by the S&D Group to change the title of the Statement by the Vice-President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on the 'Situation in the West Bank, in particular settlements' (Item 71 on the PDOJ) to the 'The situation in the West Bank, including settlements'.

The following spoke: Maria João Rodrigues, on behalf of the S&D Group, to move the request, Tomáš Zdechovský, against the request, and Maria João Rodrigues, to clarify the request.

Parliament agreed to the request by electronic vote (165 in favour, 155 against, 10 abstentions).

Request from the S&D Group to include on the agenda a motion for a resolution on the 'Situation in the West Bank, including settlements' (debate: minutes of 22.11.2016, item 11) at voting time on Thursday, 16 February 2017.

The following spoke: Maria João Rodrigues, on behalf of the S&D Group, to move the request, and Cristian Dan Preda, against the request.

Parliament rejected the request by electronic vote (142 in favour, 198 against, 3 abstentions).

Request by the EFDD Group to include on the agenda a debate on the common application of the Rules of Procedure of the European Parliament to all political groups.

The following spoke: Nigel Farage, on behalf of the EFDD Group, to move the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

Wednesday

Request by the GUE/NGL Group to include on the agenda a Commission statement on the port labour and freedom of establishment in Spanish ports as Item 4 on the agenda for the afternoon, before the debate on the report by Mady Delvaux - A8-0005/2017 (Item 13 on the PDOJ).

The following spoke: Paloma López Bermejo, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, to move the request, and Wim van de Camp, against the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

Request from the S&D Group to bring forward the debate scheduled for the Thursday afternoon on the Commission's approval of Germany's revised plan to introduce a road toll" (Item 69 on the PDOJ) and to include it as Item 5 on the agenda for the Wednesday afternoon, after the debate on the report by Mady Delvaux - A8-0005/2017 (Item 13 on the PDOJ).

The following spoke: Maria João Rodrigues, on behalf of the S&D Group, to move the request, and Françoise Grossetête, against the request.

The President drew Parliament's attention to the repercussions the vote might have on the calendar of forthcoming part-sessions.

Parliament agreed to the request by electronic vote (195 in favour, 142 against, 19 abstentions).

The vote on the motions for resolutions would take place during the March II part-session.

Thursday

In agreement with the political groups, the President proposed that Thursday's sitting open at 8.30.

Parliament agreed to the request.

° ° °

The order of business was thus established.

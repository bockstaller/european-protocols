IN THE CHAIR: Antonio TAJANI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Statements by the President

The President expressed Parliament's condolences to the families of the victims of the fire which, on 8 March 2017, swept through a youth care home in San José Pinula, Guatemala.

The President also gave an account of the celebrations to mark International Women's Day on 8 March, his participation in the extraordinary meeting of the FEMM Committee and his meeting with Sakharov Prize winners Lamiya Aji Bashar and Hauwa Ibrahim.

The President strongly condemned the Member who made unacceptable remarks during the debate on the pay gap between men and women on 1 March 2017, and confirmed that he would announce an appropriate penalty once the ongoing disciplinary procedure had finished.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 1 March 2017 and 2 March 2017 were approved.

10. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the March II 2017 sittings (PE 600.410/PDOJ) had been distributed and the following changes had been proposed (Rule 149a):

Thursday
With the agreement of the political groups, the President proposed that the debate on ‘Sudan, the cases of Hassan Abduraheem and Abdulmonen Abdumawla’ (item 80 on the draft agenda) be replaced by a debate on ‘Zimbabwe, the case of Pastor Evan Mawarire’.

Parliament approved the proposal.

Monday
Request by the ENF Group to add, as the first item on the agenda, a statement by the Vice President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on ‘Wikileaks revelations on hacking tools used by the CIA’.

The following spoke: Marcus Pretzell, on behalf of the ENF Group, to move the request.

Parliament rejected the request.

The following spoke: Kristina Winberg, to request the cancellation of the vote on Control of the acquisition and possession of weapons (Report Vicky Ford - A8-0251/2016) (item 35 on the draft agenda) (the President pointed out that the request had not been submitted in time), and Beatrix von Storch, on the title of the item listed on the agenda for Thursday afternoon as ‘Major interpellations’ (item 87 on the draft agenda) (the President confirmed that the title had been agreed by the Conference of Presidents).

Tuesday
Request by the GUE/NGL Group to add a statement by the Vice President of the Commission/High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy on ‘Continuing demolition of houses and structures owned by Palestinians’, as the third item on the agenda for that afternoon, after her statement on ‘EU priorities for the UN Human Rights Council sessions in 2017’ (item 83 on the draft agenda).

The following spoke: Neoklis Sylikiotis, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, to move the request, and Cristian Dan Preda, against the request.

Parliament rejected the request by roll-call vote (174 for, 177 against, 16 abstentions).


The following spoke: Sander Loones, to request a debate on the situation in Turkey (the President pointed out that the request had not been submitted in time), Tomáš Zdechovský, on what the previous speaker had said, and Stefan Eck, on the admissibility of the alternative motion for a resolution, submitted by the PPE, on minimum standards for the protection of farm rabbits (Report Stefan Eck - A8-0011/2017) (the President confirmed that the motion was admissible).

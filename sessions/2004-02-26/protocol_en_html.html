1. Opening of sitting

The sitting opened at 09.00.

Christian Foldberg Rovsing remarked on the time shown on one of the clocks in the hemicycle.


2. Request for defence of parliamentary immunity

The President announced that he had received on behalf of Klaus-Heiner Lehne and Martin Schulz requests to defend their privileges and immunities in relation to a court hearing in Germany.

Pursuant to Rule 6(3), these requests had been referred to the competent Committee, namely the JURI Committee.

5. Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

Charles Tannock spoke, referring to the question posed by Glyn Ford, relating to the suppression of the report commissioned by the STOA panel (Minutes of 12.02.2004, Item 5); he requested that the report be published without delay and that the President ask the Commission to undertake a comprehensive and full survey on the subject (the President replied that the President of Parliament would look into the matter).

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

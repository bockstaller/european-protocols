IN THE CHAIR: Pat COX
President

1. Opening of sitting

The sitting opened at 10.05.

The following spoke::

- Sarah Ludford, who referred to the signing by the Council on 29 April 2004 of two legislative acts without having had the opinion of Parliament, the pretext being that it had fixed a deadline that Parliament had not respected; she pointed out that a similar deadline had also been set for the proposal for a Council decision on the conclusion of an Agreement between the European Community and the United States of America on the processing and transfer of PNR data by Air Carriers to the United States Department of Homeland Security, Bureau of Customs and Border Protection [COM(2004) 190 - C5-0162/2004 - 2004/0064(CNS)], in respect of which Parliament had rejected a request for urgent procedure (Minutes of 04.05.2004, Item 7). She called on the President to send formal requests to the Council and Commission that they should abstain from taking any decisions before the Court of Justice had given its opinion;

- Johanna L.A. Boogerd-Quaak (rapporteur on the matter), who supported the previous speaker's remarks and added that she had written to the Chairman of the JURI Committee, Giuseppe Gargani, to remind him of the procedure decided on 6 April 2004; she said that she would forward this letter to the President so that, should the Council go ahead and sign the act, a different procedure could be initiated.
(The President undertook to convey Parliament's concern to the other institutions);

- Roy Perry, on a letter sent by the Chairman of the JURI Committee to the President concerning a recommendation that action should be taken against the Commission for failure to act in a matter concerning Lloyd's (the President replied that he would contact Giuseppe Gargani and provide Roy Perry with a copy of any correspondence);

- Robert Goebbels, who referred to the announcement made the previous day by the President concerning the lapsing under Rule 185 of written declarations and pending written and oral questions (Minutes of 04.05.2004, Item 6); he disputed this decision on the basis of the third paragraph of that Rule (“these provisions shall not apply to petitions and communications that do not require a decision”).

(The President replied that the announcement had been made on the basis of end-of-term precedents; however, in the light of Robert Goebbels' justifiable point, he would be calling on the Council and Commission to provide answers to the relevant questions; this would constitute a new precedent for the future in this regard).

3. Transfers of appropriations

The Committee on Budgets had considered proposal for transfer of appropriations DEC6/2004 (C5-0157/2004 - SEC(2004)0356).

After noting the Council’s opinion, it had authorised the transfer in part, pursuant to Articles 24(3) and 181(1) of the Financial Regulation of 25 June 2002.

The Committee on Budgets had considered proposal for transfer of appropriations DEC7/2004 (C5-0161/2004 - SEC(2004)0366).

After noting the Council’s opinion, it had authorised the transfer, pursuant to Articles 24(3) and 181(1) of the Financial Regulation of 25 June 2002.

5. Official welcome

On behalf of Parliament, the President welcomed Joaquim Chissano, President of the Republic of Mozambique and Chairman of the African Union, and Princess Victoria of Sweden, who had taken their seats in the official gallery.


6. Statement by the President

The President made a statement in which he drew attention inter alia to the progress achieved in the exercise of Parliament's powers during the current term, in particular in the legislative and budgetary fields, and in its development as a moving force for enlargement and the draft Treaty for a Constitution, and as a tribune for the peoples of Europe. He also took stock of his Presidency and referred to the challenges Parliament would be facing in the future.

The following paid tribute to the President: Hans-Gert Poettering, on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, Enrique Barón Crespo, on behalf of the PSE Group, Graham R. Watson, on behalf of the ELDR Group, Francis Wurtz, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, Daniel Marc Cohn-Bendit, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, who called on the President to stress to the Council and Commission that they should not take any decisions on the transfer of PNR data before the Court of Justice had delivered its opinion (the President replied that this had already been decided), Charles Pasqua, on behalf of the UEN Group, Gianfranco Dell'Alba, Non-attached Member, and Jens-Peter Bonde, on behalf of the EDD Group.


7. Membership of Parliament

The President announced that Ryszard Roman Kalisz had been appointed a member of the Polish Government.

Pursuant to Rule 8(4), his term of office as Member of the European Parliament ended with effect from 2 May 2004.

The Polish competent authorities had given notice of the appointment of Zbyszek Zaborowski to replace Ryszard Roman Kalisz, as Member of Parliament, with effect from 4 May 2004.

The President drew attention to the provisions of Rule 7(5).


8. Verification of credentials of the new Members of Parliament

Giuseppe Gargani, Chairman of the JURI Committee, gave an oral report under Rule 7(2). He indicated that the mandate of Ryszard Roman Kalisz had been validated only for the period from 1 May 2004 until his appointment to the Polish Government.

On a proposal from the JURI Committee, Parliament validated the mandates of Luis Marco Aguiriano Nalda, Nikolaos Chountis, Enric Morera Català, Jacqueline Rousseaux, Cristina Soriano Gil and José Vila Abelló, and of the 161 Members* appointed by the new Member States whose names appeared in Annex I to the Minutes of 3 May 2004.

* This number does not include the name of Ryszard Roman Kalisz, appointed to the Polish Government (see Item 7 of Minutes).

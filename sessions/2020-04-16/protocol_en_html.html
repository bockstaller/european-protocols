IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended Thursday 26 March 2020 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 9.06.


3. Statement by the President

The President gave a statement on the challenge posed by the COVID-19 pandemic and the role of Parliament and the Union vis-à-vis European citizens.

He expressed Parliament's solidarity with the victims and paid tribute to all those involved in the fight against the virus.

He stressed the need to continue the fight and to find a way out of the crisis by means of solidarity and democracy.

He also emphasised the importance of the decisions to be adopted at this extraordinary part-session with a view to coordinated action by the Union.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


5. Calendar of part-sessions

The President announced that, at the last meeting of the Conference of Presidents on Thursday 2 April 2020, he had decided, together with the political group leaders, to hold this extraordinary part-session today and tomorrow, 16 and 17 April, in order to vote on the new measures from the Commission to tackle COVID-19 and to debate and update Parliament’s position on how to combat the pandemic and its consequences.

The revised provisional timetable for 2020 was available on Parliament's website.

The fourth voting session would be open from 20.30 to 21.30, with the votes on the fourth set of amendments to the joint motion for a resolution on EU coordinated action to combat the COVID-19 pandemic and its consequences.

The results of the fourth-session votes would be announced at 22.50 and the session would be closed at 23.00.

Friday

The sitting would be opened at 9.00, with the first voting session from 9.00 to 10.00, which would include votes on amendments to:

- the two files on mobilisation of the Flexibility Instrument for 2020;

- the file on Mobilisation of the Contingency Margin in 2020;

- the three codecision files on the Corona II Package;

- Medical devices.

The results of the first-session votes would be announced at 12.30.

The second voting session would be open from 12.30 to 13.30, and will include the final votes on:

- draft amending budget No 1/2020 and draft amending budget No 2/2020;

- the two files on Mobilisation of the Flexibility Instrument for 2020;

- the file on Mobilisation of the Contingency Margin in 2020;

- the three codecision files on the Corona II Package;

- Medical devices.

- joint motion for a resolution: EU coordinated action to combat the COVID-19 pandemic and its consequences RC-B9-0143/2020.

The results of the second-session votes would be announced at 16.00.

A third voting session on amendments or the final vote on another motion for a resolution on the COVID-19 pandemic and its consequences could potentially take place from 16.00 to 17.00, and the results of the votes would be announced at 19.30.

A fourth voting session on amendments or the final vote on a further motion for a resolution on the COVID-19 pandemic and its consequences could potentially take place from 19.30 to 20.30 and the results of the votes would be announced at 22.30.

30. Corrections to votes and voting intentions

Corrections to votes and voting intentions appear on Parliament's website under 'Results of roll-call votes' as an annex to the minutes of the sitting. They are for information only and have no effect on the result of the vote as announced in plenary.

The document will be regularly updated in the two weeks following the part-session concerned.

After those two weeks, the list of corrections to votes and voting intentions will be finalised so that it can be translated and published in the Official Journal.

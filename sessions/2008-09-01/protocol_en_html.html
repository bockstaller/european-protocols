IN THE CHAIR: Hans-Gert PÖTTERING
President

1.  Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2.  Announcement by the President

The President made an announcement concerning the partial collapse of the ceiling of the Chamber in Strasbourg on 7 August 2008.

As a result of that incident, the current part-session had had exceptionally to be transferred to Brussels.


3.  Tribute

The President paid tribute on Parliament's behalf to the memory of the Members Bronisław Geremek , who had died on 13.07.2008, and Willi Piecyk , who had died on 01.08.2008, as well as to the memory of former Member Maria Luisa Cassanmagnago, who had died on 04.08.2008.

He expressed his sadness at the tragic aeroplane accidents that had occurred at Madrid-Barajas airport on 20.08.2008 and at the airport of the Kyrgyz capital Bishkek on 24.08.2008, in which many people died.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of all those who had died.

5.  Composition of Parliament

The Italian authorities had informed the Chair of the election of Innocenzo Leontini to a post incompatible with that of a Member of the European Parliament under Article 7(3) of the Act concerning the election of the members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage.

Parliament took note and, under Rule 4(1), established the vacancy of his seat with effect from 24.07.2008.

The Italian authorities had informed the Chair of the appointment of Eleonora Lo Curto to replace Innocenzo Leontini as a Member of the European Parliament with effect from 24.07.2008.

The Polish authorities had informed the Chair of the appointment of Andrzej Wielowieyski to replace Bronisław Geremek as a Member of the European Parliament with effect from 26.08.2008.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Eleonora Lo Curto and Andrzej Wielowieyski would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

7.  Interpretation of the Rules of Procedure

The President informed Parliament, under Rule 201(3) of the Rules of Procedure, of the following interpretation of Rule 182(1) provided by the AFCO Committee, to which the application of that provision had been referred.

'This Rule does not preclude the chair of the main committee from involving the chairs of the subcommittees in the work of the bureau or from permitting them to chair debates on issues specifically dealt with by the subcommittees in question – indeed, it allows this – provided that this way of proceeding is submitted to the bureau in its entirety for its consideration and that it receives the bureau’s agreement'

This interpretation would be deemed to have been adopted if no objection was raised before the minutes of the current sitting were approved.

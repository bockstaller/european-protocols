7.  Interpretation of the Rules of Procedure

The President informed the House, under Rule 201(3), of the following interpretation of Rule 116, provided by the Committee on Constitutional Affairs, which had been consulted on the application of that provision:

'The contents of a written declaration shall not go beyond the form of a declaration and shall not, in particular, contain any decision on matters for the adoption of which specific procedures and competences are laid down in the Rules of Procedure.'

If this interpretation was not contested by a political group or by at least 40 Members (Rule 201(4)) before the minutes of the current sitting were approved it would be deemed to have been adopted. Otherwise, the matter would be put to the vote in Parliament.

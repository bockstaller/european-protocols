8. Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

Anna Terrón i Cusí had notified the Presidency that she had been present at the sitting of 13 May 2003 but that her name was not on the attendance register.

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

°
° ° °

Patricia McKenna noted that a meeting of the "Bilderberg Group", of which several Commissioners had been members in the past, was due to take place the following day in Versailles; she recalled that she had asked a written question about this organisation, to which she had still not received a reply; she asked whether Commissioners took part in these meetings on a personal basis or as representatives of the Commission; she emphasised that if the latter were true, Commissioners ought to be able to inform Parliament of the meetings of the organisation, which she described as a "secret organisation"; moreover she demanded a reply from the Commission to her written question (the President undertook to forward her comments to the Commission).

13. Question Time (Council)

Parliament considered a number of questions to the Council (B5-0089/2003).

Question 1 by Alexandros Alavanos: Implementation of Council Directive 1999/70/EC by Greece.

Anastasios Giannitsis (President-in-Office of the Council) answered the question and a supplementary by Alexandros Alavanos.

Question 2 by Josu Ortuondo Larrea: War against Iraq: Aerial refuelling of B-52 bombers over European cities

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and supplementaries by Josu Ortuondo Larrea and Camilo Nogueira Román.

Marcelino Oreja Arburúa spoke on the question and Josu Ortuondo Larrea made a personal statement.

Question 3 was not called as its subject matter was already on the agenda of that part-session.

Question 4 by Bernd Posselt: Reforming the Dayton Agreement.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by Bernd Posselt.

Question 5 by Othmar Karas: European corporate tax reform policy.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and supplementaries by Othmar Karas and Paul Rübig.

Question 6 by Seán Ó Neachtain: EU initiatives to save Amina Lawal from death by stoning.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by Seán Ó Neachtain.

Astrid Thors asked a supplementary to which Anastasios Giannitsis said he would provide a written reply.

Question 7 by Niall Andrews: Cotonou Agreement and Council priorities.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by Niall Andrews.

Question 8 by Liam Hyland: WTO and agriculture deadline.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by Liam Hyland.

Question 9 by Brian Crowley: Closure of Calder Hall Magnox reactor in Sellafield.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by Brian Crowley.

Question 10 by Gerard Collins: European Rural Model.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and supplementaries by Seán Ó Neachtain and Brian Crowley.

Question 11 was not called as its subject matter was already on the agenda of that part-session.

Question 12 by Sarah Ludford: Women in Afghanistan and Iraq.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and supplementaries by Astrid Thors and Niall Andrews.

Question 13 lapsed as its author was absent.

Question 14 by José Ribeiro e Castro: Cuba: policy review.

Anastasios Giannitsis answered the question and a supplementary by José Ribeiro e Castro.

Questions which had not been answered for lack of time would receive written answers.

Council Question Time closed.

IN THE CHAIR: Rainer WIELAND

1. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 9.32.


2. Council position at first reading

The President announced that, in accordance with Rule 63(1), the following position had been received from the Council, together with the reasons which had led to its adoption, and the Commission's position on:

- Position of the Council at first reading with a view to the adoption of a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council on the establishment of a framework to facilitate sustainable investment, and amending Regulation (EU) 2019/2088 - Adopted by the Council on 15 April 2020 (05639/2/2020 - C9-0132/2020 - 2018/0178(COD)) - (02023/2020 - COM(2020)0155)
referred to committee responsible: ECON, ENVI

The three-month period available to Parliament to adopt its position would therefore begin the following day (16 May 2020).


3. First voting session

Parliament voted on:

— cosigned motion for a resolution: New MFF, own resources and Recovery plan (B9-0158/2020)

and on amendments to the following urgent procedure files:

—   Macro-financial assistance to enlargement and neighbourhood partners in the context of the Covid-19 pandemic crisis ***I [COM(2020)0163 - C9-0119/2020- 2020/0065(COD)] - Committee on International Trade;

—   Temporary measures concerning the operation of air services ***I [COM(2020)0178 - C9-0124/2020- 2020/0069(COD)] - Committee on Transport and Tourism

—   Temporary measures concerning the validity of certificates and licences (omnibus) ***I [COM(2020)0176 - C9-0126/2020- 2020/0068(COD)] - Committee on Transport and Tourism

—   Port infrastructure charges ***I [COM(2020)0177 - C9-0123/2020- 2020/0067(COD)] - Committee on Transport and Tourism

—   4th railway package: extension of the transposition periods ***I [COM(2020)0179 - C9-0125/2020- 2020/0071(COD)].

The ballot would be open until 10.45.

The vote was taken.

The voting results would be announced at 13.15.


4. Negotiations ahead of Parliament's first reading (Rule 71) (action taken)

The President announced that he had received a request for a vote at Parliament, pursuant to Rule 71(2), from the Verts/ALE Group and several Members on the AGRI Committee's decision to open interinstitutional negotiations, which had been announced in the minutes of Wednesday 13 May 2020 (minutes of 13.5.2020, item 8).

The vote would be taken today, Friday 15 May 2020 from 14.15 to 15.30.

The President announced that he had not received any requests from a number of Members or from one or more political groups meeting at least the average threshold on the decision by the PECH Committee to open interinstitutional negotiations announced in the minutes of Wednesday 13 May 2020 (minutes of 13.5.2020, item 8).

The PECH Committee had therefore been able to initiate the negotiations on expiry of the deadline laid down in Rule 71(2).

The following spoke: Bas Eickhout.

(The sitting was suspended at 9.38.)

8. Second voting session

The AGRI Committee's decision to open interinstitutional negotiations on the basis of the report on the la proposal for a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council laying down certain transitional provisions for the support by the European Agricultural Fund for Rural Development (EAFRD) and by the European Agricultural Guarantee Fund (EAGF) in the year 2021 and amending Regulations (EU) No 228/2013, (EU) No 229/2013 and (EU) No 1308/2013 as regards resources and their distribution in respect of the year 2021 and amending Regulations (EU) No 1305/2013, (EU) No 1306/2013 and (EU) No 1307/2013 as regards their resources and application in the year 2021 (COM(2019)0581 – C9-0162/2019 – 2019/0254(COD)) was put to the vote. Rapporteur: Elsi Katainen (A9-0101/2020) (minutes of 13.5.2020, item 8 and minutes of 15.5.2020, item 4).

The following spoke: Norbert Lins.

The final votes on the following urgent procedures decided on Wednesday 13 May 2020 were held (minutes of 13.5.2020, item 17):

—   Macro-financial assistance to enlargement and neighbourhood partners in the context of the Covid-19 pandemic crisis ***I [COM(2020)0163 - C9-0119/2020- 2020/0065(COD)] - INTA Committee;

—   Temporary measures concerning the operation of air services ***I [COM(2020)0178 - C9-0124/2020- 2020/0069(COD)] - TRAN Committee;

—   Temporary measures concerning the validity of certificates and licences (omnibus) ***I [COM(2020)0176 - C9-0126/2020- 2020/0068(COD)] - TRAN Committee;

—   4th railway package: extension of the transposition periods ***I [COM(2020)0179 - C9-0125/2020- 2020/0071(COD)] - TRAN Committee.

The ballot would be open until 15.30.

The voting results would be announced at 17.30.

12. Explanations of vote

Written explanations of vote:

Explanations of vote submitted in writing under Rule 194 appear on the Members' pages on Parliament's website.

Exceptionally, explanations of vote of a maximum of 400 words would be accepted.


13. Corrections to votes and voting intentions

Corrections to votes and voting intentions appear on Parliament's website under 'Results of roll-call votes' as an annex to the minutes of the sitting. They are for information only and have no effect on the result of the vote as announced in plenary.

The document will be regularly updated in the two weeks following the part-session concerned.

After those two weeks, the list of corrections to votes and voting intentions will be finalised so that it can be translated and published in the Official Journal.

IN THE CHAIR: Rainer WIELAND
Vice-President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended Thursday 30 November 2017 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.00.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The following spoke: Peter Lundgren who requested the President of Parliament not to sign the Joint Declaration on the EU's legislative priorities for 2018 and 2019 (the President took note).

°
° ° °

The minutes of the sittings of 29 and 30 November 2017 were approved.


4. Composition of Parliament

The competent French authorities had given notice of the election of Constance Le Grip to the French Parliament with effect from 1 December 2017.

This function being, pursuant to Article 7, paragraph 2 of the Act of 20 September 1976 concerning the election of the representatives of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, incompatible with occupying a post as a Member of the European Parliament, Parliament established the vacancy of the seat, on the basis of Rule 4(4) of its Rules of Procedure, with effect from 1 December 2017 and would inform the Member State concerned accordingly.

6. Composition of Parliament

The Austrian, French and German competent authorities had given notice that Lukas Mandl, Geoffroy Didier and Michael Detjen had been elected to the European Parliament with effect from 30 November 2017, 1 December 2017, and 1 January 2018 respectively, to replace Elisabeth Köstinger, Constance Le Grip, and Jutta Steinruck respectively.

Parliament noted their election.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Lukas Mandl, Geoffroy Didier and Michael Detjen would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

On a proposal from the JURI Committee, Parliament decided to validate the mandates of Wolf Klinz, Nadja Hirsch, Martin Schirdewan, Thomas Waitz and Caroline Nagtegaal, with effect from 6 November, 8 November, 8 November, 10 November and 14 November 2017, respectively.

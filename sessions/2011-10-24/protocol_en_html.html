3. Statements by the President

The President pointed out that he had attended the previous day's European Council meeting in Brussels. He then outlined the most important events that would be taking place during the week, including Wednesday's Eurogroup meeting, Thursday's presentation to Parliament of the European Council conclusions by José Manuel Barroso (President of the Commission) and Herman Van Rompuy (President of the European Council), Tuesday's debate on the European semester 2011, and Wednesday's debate and vote on the EU budget. The President also thanked those who had participated in the conference on the new multiannual financial framework for 2014-2020, which had taken place the previous Thursday.

The President expressed his satisfaction at the first free and democratic elections in Tunisia, welcoming the fact that the elections had taken place peacefully and responsibly with a turnout of over 90%. He announced that he would be visiting Tunisia the following Sunday.

The President referred to the end of the dictatorship in Libya, which had lasted more than four decades. He called on the country to establish a new political system based on the primacy of law and respect for the fundamental freedoms of all citizens. He pointed out that he would be visiting Libya the following Saturday.

The President welcomed the announcement by ETA that it was putting an end to its armed activity. He pointed out that the terrorist organisation should therefore disarm and be dissolved. He also expressed his solidarity with the families of the 829 people who had died during more than 40 years of terrorist activity.

The President stated that he shared the grief of the Turkish people following the earthquake that had occurred in the country on Sunday, killing more than 200 people. On behalf of Parliament, he expressed his condolences to the families of the victims. Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of the victims.


4. Composition of Parliament

The Swedish authorities had given notice of the election of Kent Johansson to replace Lena Ek as Member of Parliament with effect from 18 October 2011.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as his credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that he had previously made a written declaration stating that he did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Kent Johansson would take his seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

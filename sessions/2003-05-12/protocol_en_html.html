1. Resumption of session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


3. In memoriam

The President paid tribute, on behalf of Parliament, to Pierre Pradier, a former Member of Parliament, who had died on 29 April 2003.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.


4. Statements by the President (observers)

The President stated that he had received from the competent authorities of the acceding Member States the names of the 159 observers appointed to attend proceedings in the European Parliament.

The list of observers would be published in the Minutes of this sitting.

The President then made a brief statement marking the presence of those observers in the Chamber and welcoming them. He went on to welcome the success of the referendum held this weekend in Lithuania on the accession of that country to the Union.

9. Application of the Rules of Procedure

Pursuant to Rule180(3), the AFCO Committee proposed that Rule 162a, headed "Enhanced cooperation between committees", should be interpreted as follows:

"The wording of Rule 162a does not lay down any limits to its scope. Requests for enhanced cooperation between parliamentary committees concerning non-legislative reports based on Rules 163(1) and 47(1) and (2) are admissible."

That interpretation would be deemed to have been adopted if it had not been contested by the time the Minutes of this sitting were approved.


10. Judgment of the Court of First Instance in the case of Le Pen v European Parliament

On 10 April 2003, the Fifth Chamber of the Court of First Instance of the European Communities had delivered its judgment in Case T-353/00 Le Pen v European Parliament. The judgment was communicated to the European Parliament on 15 April 2003.

The Court had been called upon to rule on an action by Jean-Marie Le Pen for annulment of the act by which the European Parliament on 23 October 2000 "took note" of the disqualification of Mr Le Pen from holding office as a Member of the European Parliament.

That action was held to be inadmissible.

In accordance with Article 107(3) of the Rules of Procedure of the Court of First Instance, the suspension of the act by which Parliament had "taken note", which had been ordered as an interim measure by the President of that court on 26 January 2001, had ceased to produce any effect on 10 April 2003, at the time when the judgment was delivered, and even prior to its notification to the parties.

Consequently, the legal situation existing prior to the interim decision had been re-established in law; in other words, Parliament had already taken note of the vacancy of Mr Le Pen's seat and of the appointment of Marie-France Stirbois as a Member in his place.

The judgment of the Court of First Instance had binding force from the date of its delivery (Article 83(2) of the Rules of Procedure of the Court of First Instance).

Parliament was complying with the judgment of the Court and would comply with any future judgment, whilst fully respecting Mr Le Pen's right of appeal to the Court of Justice.

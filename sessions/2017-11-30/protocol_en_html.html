IN THE CHAIR: Antonio TAJANI
President


7. Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the recent terrorist attacks in Egypt, expressing his condolences to the victims' families and Parliament's solidarity with the Egyptian people.

He reminded the House that the fight against terrorism was a joint effort on the part of Europe and Africa, as had been highlighted at the African Union-EU summit in Abidjan.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.

8.7. 2018 budgetary procedure (Rule 150) (vote)

Report on the joint text on the draft general budget of the European Union for the financial year 2018 approved by the Conciliation Committee under the budgetary procedure [11815/2017 - C8-0313/2017 - 2017/2044(BUD)] - European Parliament delegation to the Budgetary Conciliation Committee. Rapporteurs: Siegfried Mureşan and Richard Ashworth (A8-0359/2017)

(Simple majority)
(Voting record: 'Results of votes', Item 7)

JOINT TEXT

Adopted by single vote (P8_TA(2017)0458)

The following had spoken:

Bruno Gollnisch on the procedure for requests for the waiver of Members' immunity and the forthcoming vote on the report by Laura Ferrara - A8-0363/2017 (the President reminded the House that Parliament would be voting on the report).

Günther Oettinger (Member of the Commission) and Matti Maasikas (President-in-Office of the Council), following the vote, on the 2018 budget procedure.

Before signing the budget for the 2018 financial year, the President made the following statement:

'The joint text agreed in the Conciliation Committee on 18 November 2017 has been approved by both Parliament and the Council. The budget procedure has been followed in accordance with Article 314 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union. The budget procedure for the 2018 financial year can therefore be deemed to have been completed. I declare that the Union budget for the 2018 financial year has been definitively adopted.'

The President signed the general budget in the presence of Jean Arthuis, Chair of the BUDG Committee, and Siegfried Mureşan, Richard Ashworth and Jens Geier, rapporteurs for the general budget.

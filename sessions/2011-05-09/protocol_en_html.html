IN THE CHAIR: Jerzy BUZEK
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting

The minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


3. Statements by the President

The President made the following statements.

He spoke of Europe Day and the 61st anniversary of the declaration by Robert Schuman.

He said that the United Nations had conferred a new status on the European Union, which would give it an enhanced role within that organisation.

He added that 17 May would be the seventh International Day against Homophobia.

He condemned the recent assassinations and executions in Gaza.


4. Requests to defend parliamentary immunity

Luigi de Magistris presented two requests to defend his immunity and privileges, in relation to pending court proceedings before the courts of Cosenza and Milan.

Viktor Uspaskich presented a request to defend his immunity and privileges, in relation to pending court proceedings before the court of Vilnius.

Corneliu Vadim Tudor presented a request to defend his immunity and privileges.

In accordance with Rule 6(3), these requests were referred to the committee responsible, the Committee on Legal Affairs.

The following spoke: Sergio Paolo Francesco Silvestris on Luigi de Magistris's request.


5. Composition of Parliament

The relevant Slovenian authorities had informed the European Parliament of the election of Mojca Kleva to replace Zoran Thaler, with effect from 13 April 2011.

The relevant Irish authorities had informed the European Parliament of the election of Phil Prendergast to replace Alan Kelly, with effect from 21 April 2011.

The relevant Finnish authorities had announced:

- the election of Timo Soini to the Finnish Parliament and the end of his mandate in the European Parliament with effect from 26 April 2011;

- the election to the European Parliament of Sampo Terho to replace Timo Soini, with effect from 26 April 2011.

Parliament took note of these announcements in accordance with Rule 4(1) and (4).

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as their credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that they had previously made a written declaration stating that they did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Mojca Kleva, Phil Prendergast and Sampo Terho would take their seats in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

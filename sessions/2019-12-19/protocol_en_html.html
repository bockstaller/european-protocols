4. Announcement by the President

The President made the following announcement:

"The Court of Justice of the European Union issued a judgment today in Case C-502/19, Junqueras Vies.

This is an extremely important judgment which affects directly the composition of this institution.

The Court has ruled that parliamentary office is acquired as a result of the votes cast by the electorate.

The Court has stated that when the competent authority in a Member State in which elections to the European Parliament have taken place announces officially that a person has been elected to the European Parliament, then that person immediately acquires the status of a Member of the European Parliament and benefits immediately from the protection accorded by parliamentary immunity under Article 9 of the Protocol on the Privileges and Immunities of the European Union. Consequently, the status of Member of the European Parliament must be deemed to have been acquired by the sole fact and from the very instant of this having been announced.

The Court has furthermore ruled that this immunity implies that the pre-trial detention measure to which the elected Member has been subject must now be revoked, in order to allow the Member to go to the European Parliament and complete the necessary formalities.

That said, should the national authority competent in the matter consider that the pre-trial detention should continue after the person in question has acquired the status of a Member of the European Parliament, then it must apply to the European Parliament as soon as possible for the MEP’s immunity to be waived in accordance with Article 9 of the aforementioned Protocol.

I call upon the Spanish authorities in this matter to comply with the judgment and I have authorised Parliament’s services to assess as rapidly as possible what effect this judgment will have on the composition of our Parliament.

I will not be accepting any points of order on this matter."

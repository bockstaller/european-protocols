8. Agenda

The order of business had been established (Item 8 of Minutes of 20 October 2003) and a corrigendum to the agenda had been distributed (336.401/OJ/COR).

Proposed changes:

Wednesday 5

- The President announced that he had received pursuant to Rule 144 two requests to refer the report by Joachim Wuermeling (A5-0310/2003) (Item 63 of the Agenda) back to committee: the first from the PSE Group, seeking referral before the debate, and the second from the ELDR Group, seeking referral before the vote.

The President announced that he would put these requests to the vote at the appropriate time.

The following spoke:

Astrid Thors, on behalf of the ELDR Group, who asked for the decision to be taken there and then, and Martin Schulz on behalf of the PSE Group, who supported this.

The President considered that her request was not in accordance with the Rules and decided to adhere to his initial proposal.

- To ensure that António Vitorino (Member of the Commission) could take part in the debate on the report by Jorge Salvador Hernández Mollar (A5-0361/2003) (Item 39 of the Agenda), this item was brought forward and would be taken after the debate on the report by Martine Roure (A5-0344/2003) (Item 59 of the Agenda).

Thursday 6

- The Conference of Presidents had submitted pursuant to Rule 150(2) a proposal for a decision setting up a temporary committee on improving safety at sea (B5-0468/2003). The proposal for a decision would be put to the vote at voting time on Thursday.

Parliament agreed to the changes.

The deadline for tabling amendments was set at 16:00 on Wednesday.

The following spoke:

- Neena Gill, rapporteur on the budget, who made a personal statement in response to comments made by Richard A. Balfe following the budget vote on Thursday 23 October, and Richard A. Balfe in reply;

- Klaus-Heiner Lehne, on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, who asked for the vote on the motions for resolution on market regulations and competition rules for the liberal professions (B5-0430, 0431 and 0432/2003) to be held over until the next part-session to enable a joint motion to be tabled, and Manuel Medina Ortega, on behalf of the PSE Group, who supported this.

The President established that there were no objections to this request.

- Martin Schulz, who referred to the requests for referral back to committee of the report by Joachim Wuermeling, and proposed that the report be placed at the top of that day's agenda so that a decision could be taken rapidly on the request for referral, and Joachim Wuermeling who supported this.

The President said he could not agree to this and suggested that the two political groups concerned seek a consensus which he could then put to the House after the first item on the Agenda.

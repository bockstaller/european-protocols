IN THE CHAIR: Antonio TAJANI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Statements by the President

The President made a statement on the recent terrorist attacks in London and Teheran, which had claimed many lives.

Parliament observed a minute's silence in memory of the victims.

The President spoke out on the arrest of Taner Kiliç, Chair of Amnesty International Turkey, who had been detained by police on 6 June 2017 along with 22 other lawyers, and, on Parliament's behalf, he called for their release.

The President voiced Parliament's concern over the arrest of Russian opposition leader Alexei Navalny, and also told the House that women's rights activist Loujain al Hathloul had been freed in Saudi Arabia.


3. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 31 May 2017 and 1 June 2017 were approved.


4. Composition of Parliament

The competent authorities of the United Kingdom had given notice that Anneliese Dodds, Vicky Ford, Afzal Khan and Andrew Lewer had been elected as Members of the national parliament with effect from 9 June 2017.

The House took note that, under Article7(2) of the Act concerning the election of the Members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, that office was incompatible with Membership of the European Parliament, and established the vacancy of their seats as of that date.


5. Request for waiver of immunity

The Portuguese authorities had forwarded a request to waive the immunity of Ana Gomes in connection with an investigation under way in Portugal.

Pursuant to Rule 9(1), the request had been referred to the committee responsible, the JURI Committee.


6. Composition of interparliamentary delegations

The President had received the following requests for appointments from the ALDE and ENF Groups:

Delegation for relations with the United States: Patricia Lalonde

Delegation to the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly: Thierry Cornillet

Delegation for relations with Japan: Auke Zijlstra

The appointments would be deemed ratified if there were no objections before the approval of these minutes.

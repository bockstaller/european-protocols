 ATTENDANCE REGISTER


IN THE CHAIR: Pat COX
President

1. Resumption of session

The sitting opened at 17.05.


2. Statements by the President

The President paid tribute to Anna Lindh, the Swedish Minister for Foreign Affairs and former President-in-Office of the Council, who had died on 11 September 2003 following an attack.

He noted that he had sent, in his own name and on behalf of Parliament, his condolences to Anna Lindh's family, friends and colleagues, as well as to the Swedish people.

Parliament observed a minute's silence.

Jan Andersson endorsed the President's remarks.

°
° ° °

The President welcomed the positive result of, and the high level of participation in, the Latvian referendum on accession to the European Union.


3. Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


4. Membership of committees and delegations

At the request of the PSE, PPE-DE and ELDR Groups, Parliament ratified the following appointments:

- CONT Committee: Arlene McCarthy to replace Eluned Morgan;

- EMPL Committee: Anne André-Léonard;

- AFCO Committee: Giorgio Calò to replace Paolo Costa;

- Delegation for relations with the countries of South America and MERCOSUR: Giorgio Calò;

- Delegation to the EU-Russia Parliamentary Cooperation Committee: Charles Tannock to replace Werner Langen.


5. Verification of credentials

On a proposal from the JURI Committee, Parliament validated the mandates of Peter Pex, Anne André-Léonard, Koenraad Dillen and Philip Claeys.


6. Membership of political groups

Pietro-Paolo Mennea had become a Non-attached Member with effect from 8 July 2003.

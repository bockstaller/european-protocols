4.  Belarus - the political situation after the elections (debate)

Council and Commission statements: Belarus - the political situation after the elections

Hans Winkler (President-in-Office of the Council) made the statement.

Ole Krarup expressed his disappointment that the House had been informed at such a late stage that the debate would be taking place (the President replied that the relevant provisions of the Rules had been complied with).

László Kovács (Member of the Commission) made the statement.

The following spoke: Bogdan Klich , on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, Jan Marinus Wiersma , on behalf of the PSE Group, Jeanine Hennis-Plasschaert , on behalf of the ALDE Group, Elisabeth Schroedter , on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Michał Tomasz Kamiński , on behalf of the UEN Group, Charles Tannock , Joseph Muscat , Janusz Onyszkiewicz , Konrad Szymański , Árpád Duka-Zólyomi , Rolandas Pavilionis , Ryszard Czarnecki , Hans Winkler and László Kovács .

The debate closed.

7.  Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.


8.  Supplying approved charities working to implement the European food aid programme for the most deprived citizens (written declaration)

Written declaration 75/2005 , tabled by Marie-Arlette Carlotti , Philippe Busquin , Joseph Daul , Mario Mauro and Bogusław Sonik on supplying approved charities working to implement the European food aid programme for the most deprived citizens, had, by 23.03.2006, been signed by the majority of Parliament's component Members and, under Rule 116(4), would thus be forwarded to the institutions named therein together with the names of the signatories and published in the Texts Adopted of 04.04.2006.


9.  Membership of political groups

The Chairman of the PPE-DE Group had given notice that Viorel Duca Senior had been affiliated to that group as an Observer designated by the Romanian Parliament with effect from 22.03.2006.


10.  Membership of Parliament

Şerban Nicolae, an Observer at the European Parliament designated by the Romanian Senate, had given notice of his resignation with effect from 10.04.2006. In line with the relevant rules adopted by the Bureau, Parliament would inform the Romanian authorities of the resignation in order for them to designate a replacement.

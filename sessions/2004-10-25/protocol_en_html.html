IN THE CHAIR: Josep BORRELL FONTELLES
President

1.  Resumption of session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2.  Announcement by the President

As the UN had celebrated its 59th birthday the previous day, the President emphasised, on behalf of Parliament, the important role played by the UN. He also urged the Member States to avoid disagreeing publicly within the UN.


3.  Approval of Minutes of previous sitting

Karl von Wogau and Jean-Louis Bourlanges had informed the Presidency that they had been present but that their names were not on the attendance register.

Bruno Gollnisch made a personal statement in response to the fact that he had been accused by the President, and subsequently admonished by the Bureau, for making 'revisionist comments'. He denied this and objected to the fact that he had been condemned before he had been able to respond. Mr Gollnisch went on to say that he would be seeking an apology (the President replied that, for his part, his comments had echoed the Bureau's reaction).

The Minutes of the previous sitting were approved.

8.  Approval by the Council of Parliament's positions adopted at first reading (Rule 66)

The Council had indicated, in accordance with Article 251(2) of the EC Treaty, its approval of Parliament's positions adopted at first reading:

- on 20 April 2004 with a view to adopting a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council on cooperation between national authorities responsible for the enforcement of consumer protection laws ('Regulation on consumer protection cooperation') (2003/0162(COD) );

- on 20 April 2004 with a view to adopting a directive of the European Parliament and of the Council amending the Directive establishing a scheme for greenhouse gas emission allowance trading within the Community, in respect of the Kyoto Protocol's project mechanisms (2003/0173(COD) );

- on 31 March 2004 with a view to adopting a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council amending Regulation (EC) No 1726/2000 on development cooperation with South Africa (2003/0245(COD) );

- on 31 March 2004 with a view to adopting a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council on materials and articles intended to come into contact with food and repealing Directives 80/590/EEC and 89/109/EEC (2003/0272(COD) ).

The acts in question were thus adopted. They would be signed by the President of Parliament, with the President of the Council, in accordance with Rule 68 of Parliament's Rules of Procedure.

11.  Order of business

The next item was the order of business.

The final draft agenda for the October part-session (PE 347.486/PDOJ) had been distributed. No changes had been proposed.

At the request of the Verts/ALE and GUE/NGL Groups, a deadline had been set at 19.00 that day for tabling amendments to the Emilio Menéndez del Valle report on EU-India relations (A6-0024/2004 - Item 56 on the final draft agenda ).

Council request for the application of urgent procedure (Rule 134) for:

- Proposal for a Council regulation amending Council Regulation (EC) No 2667/2000 on the European Agency for Reconstruction [COM(2004)0451 - C6-0075/2004 - 2004/0133(CNS) ]

Reason for request:

Without an extension of its mandate, the Agency would not be able to continue its work after 31 December 2004. It was thus necessary to take a decision on the extension of its mandate as soon as possible.

- Proposal for a Council regulation establishing an instrument of financial support for encouraging the economic development of the Turkish Cypriot community [COM(2004)0465 - C6-0098/2004 - 2004/0145(CNS) ]

Reason for request:

To facilitate the reunification of Cyprus, the proposal seeks to implement the conclusions of the Council on Cyprus, held on 26 April 2004, as soon as possible.

Request for urgent procedure from the Council and Commission:

- Proposal for a Council decision amending Council Decision 2002/883/EC providing further macro-financial assistance to Bosnia and Herzegovina [COM(2004)0604 - C6-0145/2004 - 2004/0207(CNS) ]

- Proposal for a Council decision on macro-financial assistance to Serbia and Montenegro and amending Decision 2002/882/EC providing further macro-financial assistance to the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [COM(2004)0605 - C6-0146/2004 - 2004/0204(CNS) ]

Reason for request:

Serbia and Montenegro and Bosnia and Herzegovina need external financial aid to continue to support the economic stabilisation and reform programmes agreed with the IMF.

Parliament would take a decision on the request for urgent procedure at the beginning of the following day's sitting.

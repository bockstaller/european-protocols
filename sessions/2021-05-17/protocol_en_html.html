IN THE CHAIR: David Maria SASSOLI
President

1. Resumption of the session

The session suspended on Thursday 29 April 2021 was resumed.


2. Opening of the sitting

The sitting opened at 17.02.


3. Statement by the President

The President made a statement on the occasion of the International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia, reiterating in particular that Parliament would defend the European Union’s values, fight discrimination and promote the rights of LGBTI persons.


4. Approval of the minutes of the previous sittings

The minutes of the sittings of 26, 27, 28 and 29 April 2021 were approved.


5. Composition of political groups

Lars Patrick Berg was no longer a member of the ID Group and sat with the non-attached Members as from 12 May 2021.

In accordance with Rule 111(6), the Chair of the Conference of Committee Chairs had notified the President of Parliament that no objection had been raised concerning:

- the recommendation of the ECON Committee to raise no objections to the Commission delegated regulation of 24 March 2021 amending Delegated Regulations (EU) No 1003/2013 and (EU) 2019/360 as regards the annual supervisory fees charged by the European Securities and Markets Authority to trade repositories for 2021 (C(2021)1874 - 2021/2617(DEA)) (B9-0265/2021);

- the recommendation of the ECON Committee to raise no objections to the Commission delegated regulation of 25 March 2021 supplementing Directive 2013/36/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council with regard to regulatory technical standards setting out the criteria to define managerial responsibility, control functions, material business units and a significant impact on a material business unit’s risk profile, and setting out criteria for identifying staff members or categories of staff whose professional activities have an impact on the institution’s risk profile that is comparably as material as that of staff members or categories of staff referred to in Article 92(3) of that Directive (C(2021)1906 - 2021/2618(DEA)) (B9-0266/2021).

If no objections were raised by a political group or a number of Members reaching at least the low threshold within 24 hours of this announcement, the recommendations would be deemed approved. If objections were raised, they would be put to the vote.

13. Order of business

The final draft agenda for the May 2021 sittings had been distributed (PE 693.081/PDOJ).

After consulting with the political groups, the President proposed the following changes to the final draft agenda:

Monday

No changes.

Tuesday

The debate on the Council and Commission statements on ‘Recent deaths in the Mediterranean and search and rescue at sea’ (item 107 on the final draft agenda) would be swapped with the debate on the Commission statement on ‘Revised industrial strategy for Europe’ (item 99 on the final draft agenda) and would thus be taken as the final item on the agenda for Tuesday morning.

The debate on the report by Tineke Strik on ‘Human rights protection and the EU external migration policy’ (A9-0060/2021) (item 79 on the final draft agenda) would be swapped with the debate on the report by Nacho Sánchez Amor on ‘2019-2020 Reports on Turkey’ (A9-0153/2021) (item 62 on the final draft agenda) and would thus be taken as the first item on the afternoon’s agenda.

Wednesday

Following the extension of the tabling deadline for motions for resolutions on the Council and Commission statements concerning ‘The right of information of the Parliament regarding the ongoing assessment of the national recovery and resilience plans’ (item 164 on the final draft agenda), the votes on these resolutions would be postponed from Wednesday to Thursday. The vote on the amendments would therefore be held in the first voting session on Thursday, with the final vote taking place in that day’s second voting session.

Thursday

No changes.

Friday

Due to the large number of amendments and requests for split and separate votes received, an additional voting session would be added on Friday from 9.45 to 11.00.

The results would be announced at 13.00.

As a consequence, the May 2021 part-session would be extended until Friday, 21 May 2021.

The President announced that no proposal to reject the Council’s position and no amendments had been tabled under Rules 67 and 68 concerning the recommendation for second reading by Clare Daly on the ‘Exchange, assistance and training programme for the protection of the euro against counterfeiting for the period 2021-2027 (Pericles IV programme)’ (item 69 on the final draft agenda).

The Council’s position was therefore deemed to have been approved. The proposed act was thus adopted (P9_TA(2021)0216).

The President pointed out that the votes would be spread over the various voting sessions depending on the number of amendments and requests for split and separate votes. Information on the manner in which the votes would be spread over the various voting sessions was available on Parliament’s website under ‘Priority information and documents’.

The House agreed to the changes.

The order of business was thus established.

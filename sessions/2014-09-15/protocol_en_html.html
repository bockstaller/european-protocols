1. Resumption of the session

The sitting opened at 17.00.


2. Announcements by the President

The President paid tribute to the former MEP Ian Paisley, who had died on Friday, 12 September 2014.

With reference to the fresh tragedies concerning immigrants in the Mediterranean, he stressed the need to do everything possible to prevent these humanitarian disasters.

The President announced that a photo session would take place in the Chamber at 12.00 on Wednesday.

4. Composition of Parliament

The Spanish authorities had given notice of the election of Tania González Peñas to replace Carlos Jiménez Villarejo as Member of Parliament with effect from 11 September 2014.

In accordance with Article 7(1) of the Act of 20 September 1976 concerning the election of the Members of the European Parliament by direct universal suffrage, and with Rule 4(1) and (4) of its Rules of Procedure, Parliament noted the election of Tania González Peñas with effect from 11 September 2014.

Under Rule 3(2), until such time as her credentials had been verified or a ruling had been given on any dispute, and provided that she had previously made a written declaration stating that she did not hold any office incompatible with that of Member of the European Parliament, Tania González Peñas would take her seat in Parliament and on its bodies and would enjoy all the rights attaching thereto.

7. Announcement by the President – Election of the European Ombudsman

The President said that a call for applications with a view to the election of the European Ombudsman had been published in the Official Journal on 2 September 2014, with the closing date for forwarding applications to the President being 30 September 2014.

The President said that, under Rule 219(2), each application had to have the support of at least 40 Members who were nationals of at least two Member States, and Members could support only one application. If a Member supported several applications, none of these choices would be taken into account. Once an application had been forwarded to the President, it would no longer be possible to withdraw or add a supporting signature.

Under Rule 219(3), applications that met all the criteria would be forwarded to the Chair of the Committee on Petitions. The Committee would hold hearings of applicants, probably in December. The hearings would be open to all Members.

The President added that the election in plenary would be held during the January 2015 part-session.

The Directorate-General for the Presidency would be able to provide Members with any further information they might require.
